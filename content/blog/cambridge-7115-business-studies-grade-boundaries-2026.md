The official grade boundaries for the Cambridge O-Level Business Studies (7115) exams in 2026 will be released on results day, which for the June session is expected around 13 August 2026. These boundaries, or **grade thresholds**, are the minimum raw marks you need to achieve a specific grade (A*, A, B, etc.) and are determined only after all exam papers have been marked. While we cannot know the exact numbers in advance, we can explain how they are set and how you can make a reliable estimate.

## What are grade boundaries?
Grade boundaries are the minimum number of raw marks a student needs to secure a particular grade for a component and for the overall qualification. They are not pre-set percentages like 90% for an A*. Instead, Cambridge International sets them after marking is complete to ensure fairness. For example, the boundary for an 'A' in Paper 1 is the lowest raw mark on that paper that is considered to be of 'A' standard. These are then combined using a weighting system to calculate the overall grade boundary for the subject.

## How Cambridge sets the 7115 boundaries
After all the marking for an exam session is finished, senior examiners and assessment experts at Cambridge analyse student performance across all papers. They compare the difficulty of the current year's exam papers against those from previous years. If a paper is found to be slightly more difficult than last year's, the grade boundaries will be lowered to compensate. Conversely, if a paper is deemed easier, the boundaries will be raised. This process ensures that the **standard** of a grade remains consistent over time, meaning it is no more or less difficult to achieve an 'A' in 2026 than it was in previous years.

## Understanding 7115 papers and marking
The Cambridge O-Level Business Studies syllabus is assessed through two papers that typically include a mix of structured questions and a longer case study. Success in these exams hinges on your ability to apply business concepts to the specific scenarios provided in the stimulus material. A generic answer, however factually correct, will not score top marks. The marking schemes heavily reward **application**, so your ability to consistently reference the business in the case is directly linked to achieving a higher raw score and comfortably clearing the boundaries for the top grades. ## Frequently asked questions

### Is it true that grade boundaries are lower if the exam is harder?
Yes, that is the core principle of the system. Cambridge adjusts grade boundaries after marking to account for the difficulty of the paper. If a paper proves to be more challenging than in previous years, the raw mark required for each grade will be lowered to ensure fairness and maintain standards.

### Can I just aim for the average boundary from the last few years?
Using the average of recent boundaries is a sensible way to set a personal target, but it is not a guarantee. Your primary focus should always be on maximising your raw mark by mastering the syllabus and exam technique, rather than just aiming for a specific number that might change.

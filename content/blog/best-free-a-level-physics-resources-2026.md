For A-Level Physics (9702), the most effective free revision strategy combines three key resources. Start with official past papers from Cambridge International, supplement your learning with the comprehensive notes on Physics & Maths Tutor (PMT), and refine your exam technique using the instant, mark-by-mark feedback from MarkScheme. This combination provides everything you need to prepare thoroughly without spending a penny.

## Start with the Source: Cambridge International
Your revision must be built on the foundation of official materials provided by Cambridge International. Past papers, mark schemes, and examiner reports are the undisputed source of truth for what to expect in your exam. They show you the precise wording of questions, the level of detail required in answers, and the common mistakes previous students have made, giving you an unparalleled insight into the examiners' minds. These are always your primary tool for practice and assessment.

## The Best Free Notes: Physics & Maths Tutor (PMT)
When it comes to free, high-quality revision notes and practice questions, Physics & Maths Tutor (PMT) is the go-to resource for most A-Level Physics students. Their notes are detailed, organised by syllabus point, and trusted by thousands. Furthermore, their extensive collection of questions categorised by topic allows you to target specific areas of weakness, making it an essential tool for both initial learning and focused revision before you move onto full past papers.

## An Effective Revision Workflow
The most successful students follow a structured revision workflow. Begin by using PMT's notes to consolidate your understanding of a topic. Next, test your knowledge with their topic-specific questions before graduating to full past papers under strict, timed conditions. The final, crucial step is to mark your paper meticulously against the official mark scheme, analysing where you lost marks and why. This cycle of 'learn, practice, test, review' is the fastest way to improve your grade.

## Smarter Marking and Feedback with MarkScheme
Marking your own work with an official mark scheme is vital, but it can be difficult to know if you've earned a specific mark, especially for definitions or calculation steps. This is where [MarkScheme's free AI marking tool](/mark) provides a unique advantage. By submitting your answers to past paper questions, you receive instant, granular feedback on each marking point, helping you understand precisely how to structure your answers for full marks—from getting the units right to including essential keywords.

## Frequently asked questions

### How should I use past papers effectively?
Simply reading past papers is not enough. You must simulate exam conditions: complete a full paper in the allotted time without notes. Afterwards, use the official mark scheme to grade your work harshly. The most important part is creating an error log, noting why you lost each mark—was it a knowledge gap, a calculation error, or a misinterpretation of the question?

### Are YouTube videos good for A-Level Physics revision?
Yes, they can be excellent for visualising abstract topics like electromagnetic fields or wave-particle duality. Channels like 'A-Level Physics Online' offer clear explanations. However, they should supplement, not replace, active revision. Watching a video is passive; you must actively solve problems from past papers to truly build exam skill and recall.

Cambridge A-Level Media Studies (9607) grade boundaries for 2026 will be published on results day, expected around 13 August 2026 for the June session. These boundaries, or **grade thresholds**, are the minimum raw marks you need to achieve a specific grade (A*, A, B, etc.) and are set after all marking is complete to ensure standards remain consistent from one year to the next. Therefore, the exact boundaries for 2026 cannot be known in advance.

## What are grade boundaries?

A **grade boundary** is the lowest possible raw mark a student can get on a paper, or in total, and still be awarded a particular grade. For example, the boundary for a grade 'A' is the minimum mark you need to score to be awarded an 'A'. It's crucial to understand that these are not pre-set percentages; they are determined by senior examiners after considering the difficulty of the examination and the overall performance of students in that session. This means the mark required for a 'B' in one year might be different from the mark required in another.

## How Cambridge sets the 9607 grade boundaries

The process of setting grade boundaries is a rigorous one designed to ensure fairness. After all exam scripts and coursework for a session have been marked, senior examiners analyse the work. They look at the difficulty of the questions and how students responded to them. Using statistical evidence and their expert judgement, they decide on the minimum quality of response that is worthy of key grades like 'A' and 'E'. This ensures that a student who achieves a grade 'A' in 2026 has demonstrated the same level of skill and understanding as a student who achieved an 'A' in a previous year, even if the papers were slightly different in difficulty. This is why the **raw mark** boundaries can, and do, move between sessions.

## Understanding your 9607 statement of results

On results day, you will receive a statement showing your raw mark for each component you took—for example, Paper 1 (Analysis of Media), Paper 2 (Media Industries and Audiences), and your coursework component. The official grade boundary document, published on the same day, will show the thresholds for each of these individual **components** as well as for the overall qualification. Your final grade is calculated by adding your weighted component marks together to get a total, which is then compared against the overall grade boundaries. This means your performance is assessed across the entire syllabus, not just on a single exam.

## Why you can't know the 2026 boundaries in advance

Predicting the exact 9607 grade boundaries for 2026 is impossible because they are not decided until after you have sat the exams and your papers have been marked. The final thresholds are a reaction to two key variables that are unknown beforehand: the precise difficulty of the exam papers and the overall performance of the entire cohort of students taking the exam in that session. If a paper turns out to be more challenging than usual, the grade boundaries may be lowered to compensate. Conversely, if a paper is more straightforward, the boundaries might be higher. This system ensures that your grade reflects your ability relative to a consistent, established standard, not the difficulty of a specific paper.

## Frequently asked questions

### Are the grade boundaries for coursework different from the exam papers?

Yes, each component has its own unique grade boundaries. The thresholds for your coursework will be different from those for Paper 1 or Paper 2, reflecting the different total marks and assessment objectives for each. Your final A-Level grade is determined by your total weighted mark from all components combined.

### Where can I find the official 2026 grade boundaries?

Cambridge International will publish the official grade threshold documents for the June 2026 exam series on their public website on results day, which is scheduled for 13 August 2026. These documents are released simultaneously to schools and the public and are not available any earlier.

### If I get a low mark on one paper, can I still get a good overall grade?

Yes, it is certainly possible. Your final grade is based on the total weighted mark from all your components. A very strong performance in your coursework or another exam paper can compensate for a weaker result in one area, as long as your total mark is high enough to meet the overall boundary for a good grade.

### How can I improve my chances of hitting a higher grade boundary?

Focus on mastering the core skills of media analysis. For 9607, this means using specialist terminology accurately, analysing how meaning is constructed with specific, detailed examples from media texts, and evaluating media industries and representations. Practising past papers and getting expert feedback is key; you can [mark a paper](/mark) with us to identify and improve on your weaknesses.

## Bottom line

The 9607 Media Studies grade boundaries for 2026 are not pre-determined and will only be finalised after the exams are marked. They are a tool to maintain fair and consistent standards across different years. Instead of worrying about specific numbers, your focus should be on developing the analytical skills required by the syllabus and using past paper boundaries as a guide to track your progress.

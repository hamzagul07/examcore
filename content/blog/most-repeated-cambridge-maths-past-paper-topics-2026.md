Maths revision feels endless until you notice the **same mark patterns**: differentiation setup, integration limits, vector geometry, and probability trees that need **working shown for M marks**. This guide ranks high-frequency topics across **9709** (A-Level), **9231** (Further Maths), and **0580** (IGCSE) from **2019—2025** sessions.

> **Key takeaway:** Maths marks come from **method + accuracy** — not a correct final number with no working.

## How this list was built

We weighted:

- **Frequency** across Pure, Mechanics, and Statistics components  
- **Mark allocation** on structured questions (not just MCQ)  
- **Examiner report** notes on lost M marks  
- **Syllabus weighting** by route (P1—P3, M1, S1, etc.)  

Confirm your **component entries** with your centre.

## A-Level Mathematics (9709)

| Priority | Topic | Why it repeats |
|----------|-------|----------------|
| 1 | **Differentiation & applications** | Stationary points, tangents, rates — M marks for setup |
| 2 | **Integration (definite & area)** | Limits, areas between curves, substitution |
| 3 | **Trigonometry & identities** | Equations, proofs, R-formula style manipulations |
| 4 | **Vectors & geometry** | Position vectors, scalar product, lines in 3D |
| 5 | **Complex numbers** | Argand diagrams, modulus-argument form |
| 6 | **Probability & distributions** | Binomial, Normal, expectation — show parameters |
| 7 | **Mechanics — forces & motion** | F=ma, resolving, connected particles |
| 8 | **Sequences & series** | AP/GP, sigma notation, convergence |

**Practise:** One multi-mark question per topic with **scheme open** — award M before A.

## Mark-scheme habits that move grades

| Trap | Fix |
|------|-----|
| Final answer only | Write **every** M-mark step the scheme lists |
| Sign errors in integration | +C and limits checked separately |
| Rounding too early | Keep exact form until final line |
| Vector diagrams missing | Sketch before algebra — examiners reward setup |

## FAQ

### Should I only revise this table?

No — cover the full syllabus once, then **weight** past papers toward these topics in the final month.

### Pure vs Mechanics vs Statistics — same priority?

Match your **entered components**. Most students overweight Pure and under-practise M1/S1 until mocks expose gaps.

### Further Maths (9231)?

See the **9231 section above** — build on 9709 Pure drills, then add matrices, induction and your specialist paper (Mechanics or Statistics).

## Bottom line

**9709** and **9231** reward **shown method**, **0580** rewards **accurate multi-step working**. The topics above appear often enough to anchor your **marked** weeks — then use [MarkScheme](/mark) to catch the M marks you still skip.

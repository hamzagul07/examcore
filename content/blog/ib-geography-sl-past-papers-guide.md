Scoring highly in IB Geography SL is not about memorising more — it is about aligning your answers with what examiners reward in the **markbands**. Strategic use of [IB Geography past papers](/ib/past-papers/geography-sl) under timed conditions, honest self-marking, and targeted feedback closes the gap between a 5 and a 7.

## Understanding the IB Geography SL assessment
**Paper 1** tests optional themes (e.g. freshwater, food, health). **Paper 2** covers the core units (populations, urban, resources). SL has no HL Paper 3 on global interactions. Browse the full subject overview at [IB Geography SL](/ib/subjects/geography-sl).

## Markbands and what examiners reward
Top answers weave **named case studies** with processes and evaluation — sustainability, stakeholder conflict, and scale (local → global).

## A past paper workflow that actually works
Case study bank per theme: location, stats, causes, impacts, management, evaluation. Practise sketch maps and annotated diagrams under time.

## Paper-specific tips
Define terms first when asked. For 9-mark questions, structure: intro → process with diagram → case study → evaluate response/management.

## Common pitfalls
Case studies without stats; confusing Paper 1 themes; no diagram when one is expected; generic "government should educate" evaluation.

## Using MarkScheme for targeted feedback

## Frequently asked questions

### How many case studies do I need?
At least one rich case per theme you study, plus flexible examples for Paper 2 cores.

### HL Paper 3 for SL students?
Not examined at SL — invest that time in case study depth.

### Fieldwork IA help exams?
Yes — methods and data presentation skills overlap with Paper 2 skills questions.

## Bottom line
Treat [IB Geography SL past papers](/ib/past-papers/geography-sl) as training for examiner thinking — not a trivia test. Cycle timed practice, honest band marking, and focused drills on your weakest criteria; that is how top candidates make a 7 predictable rather than lucky.

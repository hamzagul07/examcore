## What are grade boundaries?

Grade boundaries are minimum **raw marks** for each grade on **9696** AS and A Level components — core and advanced physical and human papers in your option route. Geography grades reward **named case-study evidence** and evaluative judgement marked to level descriptors, not theme notes alone. Each paper variant has its own threshold row in the June PDF.

## How Cambridge sets 9696 boundaries

Cambridge benchmarks scripts against level descriptors after marking, then sets thresholds so an A means the same standard each year. Paper difficulty varies — if advanced human geography essays were harshly marked, aggregate boundaries often **fall**. Compare only to past **9696** sessions, not IGCSE **0460** tables.

The International Baccalaureate grades each subject from 1 (lowest) to 7 (highest), with the full Diploma Programme being scored out of 45. This total combines your six subject scores with up to three bonus points from the Core components. Understanding how these grades are awarded is key to navigating your studies and tracking your progress towards the final exams.

## How are individual subjects graded? (1–7)
Each of your six [IB Diploma](/ib) subjects is awarded a final grade on a scale of 1 to 7. This isn't based on a fixed percentage like a typical school test. Instead, after all exams are marked, senior examiners at the IB review student work to determine the **grade boundaries** for that specific session. This means a grade 7 represents the same high standard of achievement year after year, regardless of whether a particular exam paper was slightly more or less difficult than the last.

## What are grade boundaries and how are they set?
Grade boundaries are the minimum raw marks required to achieve each grade (1–7). For example, the boundary for a grade 6 is the lowest total mark a student can get on all their assessed components (exams and coursework) and still be awarded a 6. This process, known as **awarding**, is done for every subject in every exam session (May and November). Senior examiners analyse scripts at the borderline of each grade to find the 'tipping point', ensuring fairness and maintaining consistent standards across different years and cohorts. This is why you cannot simply aim for "85%" to get a 7; the required mark changes.

## The full Diploma score out of 45
The famous 'out of 45' score is a combination of your subject performance and your work in the IB Core. The scores from your six subjects (each graded 1–7) are added together for a maximum of 42 points (6 subjects × 7 points). The remaining three points are awarded based on your performance in **Theory of Knowledge (TOK)** and the **Extended Essay (EE)**. Your grades in these two components (A to E) are combined in a matrix to give you 0, 1, 2, or 3 bonus points. A student who achieves an A in both TOK and the EE will receive the full 3 points.

## What's the passing score for the IB Diploma?
To be awarded the IB Diploma, a student must achieve a minimum of **24 points**. However, simply hitting this number isn't enough; there are several other conditions you must meet. These include avoiding certain low grades (e.g., no grade 1s, and a limited number of 2s and 3s across your subjects) and satisfactorily completing all Core requirements. You must achieve at least a grade D in both Theory of Knowledge and the Extended Essay to be eligible for the Diploma at all. Failing to submit a CAS portfolio is also an automatic failure condition.

## Why you can't rely on old percentages
A common mistake is to look at a past grade boundary report and assume the same percentages will apply to your exams. Because boundaries are set after marking to account for paper difficulty, they fluctuate. A 75% might earn a grade 7 in a difficult Maths paper one year, but only a 6 in a more straightforward one the next. This is why your focus should be on mastering the syllabus content and exam technique, not on hitting a magic number. Use [IB past papers](/ib/past-papers) and [topic practice](/ib/past-papers/biology-hl#ib-topic-practice) to drill syllabus points, and get expert [feedback on your work](/mark) to understand the qualitative standards expected for the top grades — far more valuable than calculating a percentage.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is a 7 the same as an A*?
Broadly, yes. A grade 7 is the highest available and is recognised by universities worldwide as equivalent to an A* at A-Level. However, the grading philosophy is different. The IB's 1–7 scale provides more points of differentiation than the A*–E scale, and the criteria for achieving each grade are defined by qualitative markbands, not just a percentage.

### Where can I find the official grade boundaries?
The IB publishes official grade boundary documents for each examination session (May and November) after results are released. Your school's IB Coordinator will have access to these, and they are often shared widely online. Remember, these are a historical record and only a rough guide for future sessions, not a fixed target.

### How do I know what a 'grade 6' answer looks like?
The quality of an answer is defined by **markbands** or level descriptors in the official mark scheme for each question. These describe the characteristics of a typical response at each level. The best way to understand this is to study mark schemes alongside [IB past papers](/ib/past-papers) and, crucially, to get your own work assessed by a teacher or tutor who can explain why your response fits into a particular markband.

## Bottom line
Your IB grades are not determined by a simple percentage curve but by a rigorous process that ensures a 7 in Physics or a 6 in History represents a consistent standard of excellence over time. The final Diploma score out of 45 reflects your academic breadth across six subjects and your engagement with the Core. Focus on mastering the skills and knowledge for your subjects, and the points will follow.

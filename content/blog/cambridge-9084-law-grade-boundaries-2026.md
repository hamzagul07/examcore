The official grade boundaries for the Cambridge International A-Level Law (9084) 2026 exam series will be published on results day, which for the June 2026 session is 13 August 2026. These thresholds are the minimum raw marks required for each grade and are determined *after* all papers have been marked to ensure standards remain consistent year-on-year. While you cannot know the exact 2026 boundaries in advance, you can make a strong estimate by comparing your practice paper performance against the boundaries from recent past sessions.

## What are grade boundaries?
A grade boundary, also known as a **grade threshold**, is the minimum raw mark a student must achieve on a paper or in total to be awarded a specific grade (e.g., A, B, C). Cambridge International does not decide these numbers in advance. Instead, senior examiners review student performance and the difficulty of the exam papers after marking is complete. This process ensures that it is no more or less difficult to achieve a certain grade than it was in a previous year, maintaining a fair and consistent standard over time.

## How 9084 Law papers are marked
Your raw mark, which is compared against the grade boundary, is awarded based on a 'levels of response' mark scheme. For scenario-based problem questions, examiners look for your ability to follow the IRAC (or ILAC) structure: identifying the legal **Issue**, stating the relevant **Rule** of law with authority, **Applying** the law to the facts of the scenario, and drawing a logical **Conclusion**. For essay questions, high-level marks are awarded for well-structured arguments that are supported by relevant legal **authority** (cases and statutes) and demonstrate **balance** by considering counter-arguments or different legal perspectives.

## Why grade boundaries change each year
Grade boundaries for 9084 Law fluctuate between exam sessions for two primary reasons: variations in paper difficulty and the performance of the student cohort. If an exam paper is deemed slightly more challenging than the previous year's, the grade boundaries may be lowered to compensate. Conversely, if a paper is more straightforward, the boundaries might be raised. This flexibility is essential to uphold the standard of an A-Level grade, ensuring that an 'A' in 2026 represents the same level of achievement as an 'A' in any other year.

## Component vs. overall thresholds
When Cambridge releases grade boundaries, they provide two tables. The **component** threshold table shows the minimum mark needed for each grade on each individual paper (e.g., Paper 1, Paper 2, etc.). The **overall** threshold table shows the minimum total weighted mark needed across all components to achieve your final qualification grade. Your final A-Level grade is determined by the sum of your marks, so strong performance on one paper can help compensate for a weaker performance on another.

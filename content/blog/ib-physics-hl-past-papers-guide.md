Scoring highly in IB Physics HL is not about memorising more — it is about aligning your answers with what examiners reward in the **markbands**. Strategic use of [IB Physics past papers](/ib/past-papers/physics-hl) under timed conditions, honest self-marking, and targeted feedback closes the gap between a 5 and a 7.

## Understanding the IB Physics HL assessment
**Paper 1** MCQ. **Paper 2** structured and extended across mechanics, fields, waves, and atomic physics including AHL. **Paper 3** practical-style and option questions. Browse the full subject overview at [IB Physics HL](/ib/subjects/physics-hl).

## Markbands and what examiners reward
Extended answers need **physics reasoning**: diagrams, stated assumptions, SI units, and linking maths to physical meaning.

## A past paper workflow that actually works
Alternate full papers with AHL topic drills. Build your own formula sheet; practise explaining why an equation applies.

## Paper-specific tips
Draw force and circuit diagrams. Combine uncertainties correctly on Paper 3.

## Common pitfalls
Sign errors; scalar/vector confusion; skipping unit conversion; neglecting the option.

## Using MarkScheme for targeted feedback
Self-marking against band descriptors is essential, but extended responses benefit from a second opinion. After a past paper or IA section, [get criterion-based feedback](/mark?subject=ib-physics-hl) aligned with IB assessment objectives — the same habits that lift exam scripts also sharpen coursework drafts.

Scoring highly in IB History SL is not about memorising more — it is about aligning your answers with what examiners reward in the **markbands**. Strategic use of [IB History past papers](/ib/past-papers/history-sl) under timed conditions, honest self-marking, and targeted feedback closes the gap between a 5 and a 7.

## Understanding the IB History SL assessment
**Paper 1** is source-based on a prescribed subject. **Paper 2** requires two essays from different world history topics. SL has **no Paper 3** — regional depth is HL only. The **IA** is a historical investigation. Browse the full subject overview at [IB History SL](/ib/subjects/history-sl).

## Markbands and what examiners reward
Essays need argument, evidence, and historiography — not narrative. Paper 1 rewards OPVL (origin, purpose, value, limitation) applied to the question.

## A past paper workflow that actually works
Paper 1: practise every question type in the booklet. Paper 2: prepare two topic areas with essay plans and specific evidence banks (dates, names, statistics).

## Paper-specific tips
Paper 1 synthesis question — integrate sources **and** own knowledge. Paper 2 — address the command term in your thesis first sentence.

## Common pitfalls
Storytelling essays; generic OPVL without linking to utility; only revising one Paper 2 topic; running out of time on the second essay.

## Using MarkScheme for targeted feedback
Self-marking against band descriptors is essential, but extended responses benefit from a second opinion. After a past paper or IA section, [get criterion-based feedback](/mark?subject=ib-history-sl) aligned with IB assessment objectives — the same habits that lift exam scripts also sharpen coursework drafts.

## Frequently asked questions

### How many Paper 2 topics to learn?
You need depth in at least two world history topics — check which two your school entered.

### Can I use HL Paper 3 questions?
Not for SL exams — but HL-style depth of evidence helps Paper 2 essays.

### Best Paper 1 drill?
Timed source comparisons with a strict paragraph formula: claim → source evidence → limitation → judgement.

## Bottom line
Treat [IB History SL past papers](/ib/past-papers/history-sl) as training for examiner thinking — not a trivia test. Cycle timed practice, honest band marking, and focused drills on your weakest criteria; that is how top candidates make a 7 predictable rather than lucky.

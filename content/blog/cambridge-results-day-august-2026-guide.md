## How to access your results

1. **School candidates** — your exams officer distributes login details or printed statements. Process varies by centre.  
2. **Portal** — many students use [Cambridge Candidate Results](https://myresults.cie.org.uk) with credentials from your centre.  
3. **Private candidates** — collect results through your registered exam centre (e.g. British Council), not directly from Cambridge.

On release morning, the portal can be **slow for the first 30 minutes**. Have your candidate number and password ready the night before.

### What to download immediately

- **Statement of Results** (PDF) — save two copies  
- Note **syllabus codes**, **grades**, and **percentage uniform marks (PUM)** where shown  
- Screenshot is not enough for official applications — keep the PDF  

## Reading your grades (without misreading them)

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| Grade (A*, A, B…) | Official outcome for that syllabus |
| PUM (0–100) | Where you sit **inside** the grade band — not the same as raw paper % |
| Component grades | Per-paper breakdown when issued |
| X / Q / Pending | Incomplete marking or administrative hold — contact exams officer |

A **B with PUM 79** is not “almost an A” on a certificate — but it tells you improvement is close. See [grade boundaries for past-paper students](/blog/cambridge-grade-boundaries-past-papers) for how thresholds relate to revision.

> **Key takeaway:** One grade is a snapshot. Your **error patterns** from marked past papers usually predicted the outcome — use them for the next move.

## If results meet or beat your target

| Next step | Why |
|-----------|-----|
| Save PDFs + back up email | University and visa applications |
| Thank teachers / note what worked | Repeatable study habits matter for A2 |
| Light rest before next term | Burnout after results week is real |

If you are continuing to A2 or university prep, keep one **marked past paper per subject** as a baseline — not to cram, but to remember what honest marking felt like.

## If results miss your target

Do **not** post your full statement publicly or argue with teachers on results morning. Use this order:

### Hour 0–24: breathe and collect facts

- List subjects **below** offer requirements  
- Note components with **surprisingly low** marks vs your mocks  
- Read [retakes and resits strategy](/blog/cambridge-retakes-and-resits-2026-strategy)  

### Hour 24–48: talk to your exams officer

Ask specifically:

- Is a **clerical check** or **priority re-mark** appropriate?  
- What is the **school’s internal EAR deadline**? (Often earlier than Cambridge’s)  
- Can you enter **November 2026** resits for which components?  

Full process: [Enquiry about Results guide](/blog/cambridge-enquiry-about-results-ear-guide-2026).

### Week 1: choose one path per subject

| Path | When it fits |
|------|--------------|
| Accept and move on | Grade meets minimum for your next step |
| EAR (remark) | One component clearly below marked mock trend |
| November resit | Whole subject or single component retake planned |
| Re-mark + resit backup | Borderline university offer |

## Enquiries about results deadline (June series)

Cambridge’s final deadline for Enquiries about Results from the **June 2026** series is **20 September 2026**. Schools often set **mid-August** internal cut-offs — missing your centre’s date cannot be fixed by Cambridge.

## Planning revision before November resits

If you are resitting:

1. Request **access to your marked scripts** where your centre allows post-EAR review  
2. Re-mark your June paper against the scheme — no generosity  
3. Build a 10-week plan from [4-week sprint structure](/blog/how-to-revise-cambridge-exams-in-4-weeks), extended  
4. Upload weak answers to [MarkScheme](/mark) weekly if you lack a teacher

## FAQ

### When are November 2025 series results if I sat then?

November series results typically release in **mid-January** (AS/A-Level first, IGCSE/O-Level a few days later). June 2026 dates above do not apply to that series.

### Can I appeal directly to Cambridge?

Students apply through **schools or registered centres** via Cambridge International Direct — not by emailing Cambridge yourself. Private candidates use their exam centre.

### Do grade boundaries come out on results day?

**Grades** and **grade threshold tables** are not always released at the same moment. For June 2026, AS & A Level **grades** publish **11 August**; **threshold tables** for the series typically follow around **13 August**. IGCSE and O Level **grades** publish **18 August**. Use our [grade boundaries hub](/guides/grade-boundaries) once thresholds are live.

### How do I practise for a resit now?

Same syllabus, same mark schemes. Start with [how many past papers](/blog/how-many-cambridge-past-papers-before-exams) and mark every one.

## Bottom line

**11 August** for AS/A-Level, **18 August** for IGCSE/O-Level — then pause, save your PDFs, and speak to your exams officer within 48 hours if something looks wrong. Whether you celebrate or resit, the students who recover fastest go back to **marked past papers**, not vague revision.

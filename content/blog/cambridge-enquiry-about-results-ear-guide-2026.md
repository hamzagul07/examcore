You opened your statement and one grade **does not match** how your papers felt. Cambridge offers **Enquiries about Results (EAR)** — but a remark is expensive, slow, and can move your grade **down** as well as up. Here is how EAR works in 2026 and when marking more past papers beats paying for a re-mark.

## Quick answer

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| Can I apply myself? | **No** — school exams officer or your exam centre submits |
| June 2026 series final deadline | **20 September 2026** (centre deadlines often earlier) |
| Can my grade go down? | **Yes** — you must consent before a re-mark |
| Refund if grade rises? | Often yes for priority re-mark fees — confirm with centre |
| Best first step? | **Clerical check** if you suspect adding errors |

Source: [Cambridge EAR help article](https://help.cambridgeinternational.org/hc/en-gb/articles/115004310349-Enquiries-about-Results-General-Queries).

## Types of Enquiry about Results

| Service | What it checks | Typical use |
|---------|----------------|-------------|
| **Clerical re-check** | Totals, transcription, option codes | “My marks do not add up” |
| **Review of marking** (priority / standard) | Examiner re-reads script against scheme | “I was marked harshly on essays / working” |
| **Review of moderation** | Internally assessed work (coursework) | Centre-assessed components only |

Cambridge processes enquiries in order received and aims to respond within **30 days** of receipt. Peak August–September queues happen — apply early if your centre agrees.

## June 2026 key deadlines

| Milestone | Date |
|-----------|------|
| AS/A-Level results released | 11 August 2026 |
| IGCSE/O-Level results released | 18 August 2026 |
| **Final EAR deadline (June series)** | **20 September 2026** |

British Council and school **internal deadlines** are often **mid-August to early September**. Treat the first week after results as your real window.

## Who applies and how

### School candidates

1. Speak to your **exams officer** within **48 hours** of results  
2. Provide specific papers/components — not “remark everything”  
3. Sign consent forms (grade can decrease)  
4. Pay fees through school where required  

### Private candidates

Apply through your **registered exam centre** (e.g. British Council School Registration System). You cannot bypass the centre and email Cambridge directly.

Exams officers submit via **Cambridge International Direct**.

## Fees (indicative — confirm with centre)

Fees vary by zone and service level. Centres often quote roughly:

- **Clerical check** — lower fee per component  
- **Priority re-mark** — higher fee; faster turnaround before university deadlines  
- **X / Q / Pending** queries — often handled separately; ask officer  

If your grade **increases**, many centres refund the re-mark fee. If it stays the same or drops, you typically pay.

## When a remark is reasonable

| Signal | Remark may help |
|--------|-----------------|
| One component far below **honestly marked** mocks | Yes — if gap is 2+ grade bands |
| Clerical error suspected (total ≠ parts) | Yes — start with clerical check |
| Whole subject weak across components | Unlikely — systematic gap, not one harsh marker |
| You never saw mark schemes before sitting | Risky — examiners may have been fair |
| Borderline university offer, one paper carries offer | Discuss priority re-mark with officer |

> **Key takeaway:** Compare results to **strict self-marked past papers**, not to how you “felt” in the exam hall.

## When a remark is a bad bet

- You did not complete questions — marks lost to **blank pages**, not harsh marking  
- Weak mocks all year — results confirm the trend  
- You want “one more try” without evidence — see [retakes guide](/blog/cambridge-retakes-and-resits-2026-strategy) instead  
- Social media says “everyone should remark” — ignore  

## What to do while waiting for EAR outcome

Do not freeze revision:

1. Re-mark your June paper from memory + scheme where allowed  
2. Log **three repeatable errors** (not “bad day”)  
3. Start November resit planning in parallel — EAR and resit are not mutually exclusive  
4. Practise on [MarkScheme](/mark) if you lack teacher feedback  

## After EAR: grade up, same, or down

| Outcome | Next step |
|---------|-----------|
| **Up** | Update university / counsellor immediately; save new statement |
| **Same** | Accept or plan November/June resit with new method |
| **Down** | Rare but possible — only consent if evidence was strong |

If remark fails, the fix is **more marked past papers**, not another paid service immediately.

## EAR vs appeals

**EAR** reviews marking of sat exam scripts. **Appeals** (stage 1 / stage 2) are a separate process for procedural issues — schools submit on your behalf. Do not confuse TikTok “appeal templates” with standard EAR.

## FAQ

### How long does a Cambridge re-mark take?

Cambridge targets **30 days** from receiving the enquiry. Priority services exist for university deadline pressure — ask your officer.

### Can I remark one paper only?

Yes — and you should. Select components with the largest gap vs expected performance.

### Will I see my marked script?

Policy varies by centre and service. Some schools share feedback post-EAR; others do not. Ask early.

### Does MarkScheme replace a remark?

No — [MarkScheme](/mark) helps you **practise** for resits and understand schemes. It does not change awarded grades.

## Bottom line

EAR is a **surgical tool** for likely clerical errors or one surprisingly low component — not a lottery ticket. Move fast (centre deadlines beat Cambridge’s 20 September), consent with eyes open, and keep revising for a resit backup. Long-term, students who climb grade bands do it through **honest marking habits**, not repeated remarks.

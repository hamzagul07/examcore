Cambridge **Accounting** (9706) is one of the most searched A-Level subjects for **past papers** and **mark scheme** help. This guide explains how the syllabus is examined, how marks are awarded, and how to turn practice into real marks — not just completed pages.

Past papers are labelled **9706/XX** where **XX** is the component (paper) number for that session. Always check you are practising the right **component** for your route.

## How the papers are structured

Structured papers with financial statements, ratios, and decision-making topics. Accuracy and format matter.

Download papers from Cambridge International or your school portal, then mark with the **official mark scheme** for that exact session and question number.

## How mark schemes work for Accounting

Marks for correct figures, labels, and treatments (e.g. accruals, depreciation). Working often required for calculation marks.

When you **self-mark**, read the scheme before you look at your answer — otherwise you unconsciously accept partial credit.

## Common mistakes students make on 9706

- Wrong account titles in ledger questions
- Arithmetic slips without clear workings
- Confusing cash flow vs profit impacts

If the same mistake appears twice in one week of marking, it is a **revision priority**, not bad luck.

## A practical revision plan with past papers

- Practice full financial statement questions timed
- Drill ratio interpretation with past data
- Mark line-by-line against the scheme

**Rule of three:** attempt → mark with scheme → rewrite the weakest part. Skipping step two is why students feel they are "doing past papers" without grades moving.

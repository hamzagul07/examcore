## Quick answer

**Save My Exams** is strong for organised **notes and topic questions** (mostly paid). **MarkScheme** ([markscheme.app](https://markscheme.app)) is built for **second-pass marking from handwriting** against Cambridge mark schemes and IB markbands, plus **free courses** on both tracks. They solve different jobs — many students use PMT/ZNotes for notes and MarkScheme for marking. Full table: [/compare](/compare).

## What this demo shows

This video answers the question we get daily: "Is MarkScheme just Save My Exams?" It is not. Save My Exams bundles revision content; MarkScheme focuses on whether your **handwritten answer** earns marks on the real scheme. The demo walks through one past-paper question on each approach.

## Comparison at a glance

| | Save My Exams | MarkScheme |
|---|---------------|------------|
| **Best for** | Notes, topic Q banks | Marking your handwriting |
| **Marking** | Limited AI on short answers | B1/M1/A1 + essay bands + IB markbands |
| **Courses** | Paid notes | Free Cambridge + IB syllabus courses |
| **Cost** | Freemium / subscription | Free tier for marking |
| **Honest limit** | Not a full script marker | Not a notes library |

## Step-by-step (from the video)

1. **Notes phase** — use Save My Exams, PMT, or ZNotes to learn the topic (MarkScheme does not replace this).
2. **Attempt** — write a timed handwritten answer.
3. **Self-mark** — official PDF mark scheme or IB markband column.
4. **Second pass** — upload the same script to [markscheme.app/mark](/mark) for scheme-aligned feedback.
5. **Decide** — if you only need condensed notes, Save My Exams may be worth it; if you need marking practice, MarkScheme fills the gap.

## Honest limits

Neither tool replaces official past papers and mark schemes from Cambridge or the IBO. Save My Exams depth is behind a paywall for many subjects. MarkScheme does not host the same note library. We are not endorsed by Cambridge International or the IBO. Use [/compare](/compare) before you assume one product replaces the other.

The official grade boundaries for the Cambridge International A-Level Chemistry (9701) 2026 exams will be released on results day, which for the June 2026 session is 13 August 2026. These **grade boundaries**, or thresholds, are the minimum raw marks you need to achieve a particular grade (A*, A, B, etc.) and are determined after all papers have been marked. They are not known in advance because they are adjusted each year to account for the difficulty of the examination papers.

## What are grade boundaries?
A **grade boundary** is the minimum raw score a candidate needs to secure a specific grade for a paper (**component**) or for the qualification as a whole. For example, the boundary for a grade 'A' is the lowest mark that Cambridge considers to represent A-grade standard for that particular exam paper. Cambridge sets these **thresholds** after the marking process is complete to ensure that the standard required for a grade remains consistent from one year to the next.

## How are 9701 Chemistry papers marked?
Your final A-Level Chemistry grade is an aggregate of your performance across several papers: Paper 1 (Multiple Choice), Paper 2 (AS Level Structured Questions), Paper 4 (A2 Level Structured Questions), and either Paper 3 (Advanced Practical Skills) or Paper 5 (Planning, Analysis and Evaluation). Each question is awarded a specific number of **raw marks**. For structured questions in Papers 2 and 4, precision is crucial; marks are often awarded for correct chemical equations with state symbols, accurate use of units, and correctly drawn curly-arrow mechanisms.

## Why do grade boundaries change each year?
Grade boundaries for 9701 Chemistry are not fixed; they fluctuate between exam sessions (e.g., June 2025 vs. November 2025 vs. June 2026). This is a core part of ensuring fairness. If a particular exam paper is found to be more challenging than in previous years, the grade boundaries will be lowered to compensate. Conversely, if a paper is less demanding, the boundaries will be raised. This system ensures that a student who achieves a grade 'A' in 2026 has demonstrated the same level of chemical knowledge and skill as a student who achieved an 'A' in any other year.

## When will the 2026 grade boundaries be released?
Cambridge International publishes the official grade boundary tables for each exam session on results day. For the June 2026 series, this will be on 13 August 2026. The thresholds are released for each individual **component** (e.g., Paper 12, Paper 22, etc.) and for the overall qualification options (e.g., AS Level, A Level). Until this date, any figures you see online are purely estimates based on historical data.

## Frequently asked questions

### Are the grade boundaries for AS and A Level the same?
No, they are distinct. The AS Level grade (a to e) is based on your performance in the AS components only (typically Papers 1, 2, and 3). The A Level grade (A* to E) is based on your performance across all components, both AS and A2. The raw mark required for an 'A' at A Level is significantly higher than for an 'a' at AS Level.

### If I get a low mark on one paper, can I still get a good overall grade?
Yes, it is possible. Your final grade is determined by the sum of your weighted marks across all papers, not your grade on a single component. A very strong performance on the structured papers (Papers 2 and 4), for example, can help compensate for a weaker score on the multiple-choice paper. The key is your total weighted mark.

### What are 'component' and 'option' codes on the boundary document?
A **component** code refers to a specific exam paper (e.g., '12' for Multiple Choice Paper 1, Variant 2). An **option** code refers to the combination of papers you took for your final qualification (e.g., 'AX' for the full A Level). When checking boundaries, ensure you are looking at the correct codes for the papers you sat.

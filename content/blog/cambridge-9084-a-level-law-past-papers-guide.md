Past papers are labelled **9084/XX** where **XX** is the component (paper) number for that session. Always check you are practising the right **component** for your route.

## How the papers are structured

Scenario problem questions and essay papers. Application of legal rules to facts is central.

Download papers from Cambridge International or your school portal, then mark with the **official mark scheme** for that exact session and question number.

## How mark schemes work for Law

Issue → rule → application → conclusion (IRAC-style) for problems. Essays need authority (cases/statutes) and balanced argument.

When you **self-mark**, read the scheme before you look at your answer — otherwise you unconsciously accept partial credit.

## Common mistakes students make on 9084

- Stating law without applying to facts
- Missing opposite party arguments
- No conclusion on problem questions

If the same mistake appears twice in one week of marking, it is a **revision priority**, not bad luck.

## A practical revision plan with past papers

- Drill case names and ratios weekly
- Practice problem questions with timed plans
- Mark application paragraphs separately from rule statements

**Rule of three:** attempt → mark with scheme → rewrite the weakest part. Skipping step two is why students feel they are "doing past papers" without grades moving.

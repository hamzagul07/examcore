## What are grade boundaries?

Grade boundaries are the minimum **raw marks** Cambridge requires for each letter grade on each component of **4024 Mathematics**. They are not fixed percentages — your **overall** O-Level grade uses a weighted aggregate across the papers you entered, and each paper variant has its own row in the official PDF. Match your exact component codes from the statement of entry before comparing marks to any past session.

## How Cambridge sets 4024 boundaries

Cambridge sets **4024** thresholds **after** all scripts are marked so each grade means the same standard every year. Calculator and non-calculator papers both reward **shown method** — skipping steps loses M marks on structured questions. When a paper is harder than the previous June series, raw cut-offs usually **fall** — comparable outcomes, not fixed quotas. Compare only to verified **4024** sessions ([wrong-session estimates](/insights)).

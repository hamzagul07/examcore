The official grade boundaries for the Cambridge O-Level Additional Mathematics 4037 exam in 2026 will be published on results day, 13 August 2026. These boundaries, known as **grade thresholds** by Cambridge, are the minimum raw marks you need for each grade (A*, A, B, etc.) and are set *after* all papers have been marked. This process ensures that standards are consistent from one year to the next, meaning the difficulty of getting a particular grade remains fair regardless of minor variations in the exam papers.

## What are grade boundaries?

In simple terms, a grade boundary is the lowest possible raw mark you can get on your exam papers and still be awarded a specific grade. For example, the boundary for a grade 'A' is the minimum total mark across both papers that a student must achieve to secure that A. These are not percentages set in advance; they are precise mark totals determined by senior examiners after a rigorous review of student performance across the globe. This ensures that the standard for an 'A' in one session reflects the same level of achievement as an 'A' in any other session.

## How Cambridge sets the 4037 boundaries

The process of setting boundaries is a cornerstone of maintaining fair and reliable qualifications. After all 4037 papers are marked, Cambridge's assessment experts analyse the results. They look at how students performed on each question and on the papers as a whole, comparing this performance to previous years. If a paper is judged to have been slightly more challenging than the previous year's, the grade boundaries may be lowered to compensate. Conversely, if a paper was more straightforward, the boundaries might be slightly higher. This statistical analysis, combined with expert academic judgement, ensures that a student's final grade reflects their subject mastery, not the specific difficulty of the paper they happened to sit.

## Understanding the 4037 Additional Mathematics papers

Cambridge 4037 Additional Mathematics is designed as a crucial bridge between O-Level Mathematics and the more advanced concepts required for A-Level. The syllabus covers demanding topics in algebra, functions, coordinate geometry, trigonometry, and an introduction to calculus. Your overall grade is determined by your performance across two written papers, Paper 1 and Paper 2, which are weighted equally. Cambridge sets a grade threshold for each individual paper and then calculates the overall syllabus threshold. This means your final grade depends on your combined **raw mark** from both components, and it is this overall mark that is compared against the final grade boundaries.

## Why grade boundaries change each year

It is a common misconception that grade boundaries should be static. In fact, their ability to change is what makes the system fair. The primary reason boundaries fluctuate between exam sessions (e.g., June 2025 vs June 2026) is to account for slight differences in the difficulty of the examination papers. It is impossible to create two exam papers with the exact same level of difficulty. By adjusting the boundaries up or down, Cambridge ensures that you are not disadvantaged by a particularly hard paper or advantaged by a slightly easier one. The goal is always to maintain the standard of achievement required for each grade over time.

They review scripts at the borderline for each grade to determine the minimum standard of work that is worthy of that specific grade.

Scoring highly in IB English A: Language and Literature HL is not about memorising more — it is about aligning your answers with what examiners reward in the **markbands**. Strategic use of [IB English A: Language and Literature past papers](/ib/past-papers/english-a-lang-lit-hl) under timed conditions, honest self-marking, and targeted feedback closes the gap between a 5 and a 7.

## Understanding the IB English A: Language and Literature HL assessment
**Paper 1** analyses unseen non-literary texts. **Paper 2** compares two literary works. **HL Essay** (1,200–1,500 words) on a literary work. **Individual Oral** links a global issue to works studied. Browse the full subject overview at [IB English A: Language and Literature HL](/ib/subjects/english-a-lang-lit-hl).

## Markbands and what examiners reward
Criteria reward interpretation, analysis of authorial choices, coherence, and language — HL expects greater depth and range than SL.

## A past paper workflow that actually works
Paper 1: technique → effect → purpose chains. Paper 2: balance both texts each paragraph. HL Essay: one sharp thesis, no plot summary.

## Paper-specific tips
Avoid feature spotting. IO: rehearse with the same note policy as the exam.

## Common pitfalls
Plot summary; HL Essay drafted late; IO global issue too vague.

## Frequently asked questions

### HL vs SL?
HL adds HL Essay and longer IO — criteria align but expectations are higher.

### Lang & Lit vs Literature?
Lang & Lit Paper 1 is non-literary; Literature analyses unseen literary extracts.

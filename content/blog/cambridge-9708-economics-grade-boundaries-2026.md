The official Cambridge 9708 Economics grade boundaries for 2026 will be released on results day, 13 August 2026. They are not predetermined percentages but are set after marking to ensure fairness across different exam sessions. Understanding how these boundaries work is key to tracking your progress and knowing what to aim for.

## What are grade boundaries?
A grade boundary, or 'grade threshold', is the minimum raw mark you need to achieve a particular grade on a paper or for the overall qualification. For example, the boundary for a grade 'A' is the lowest raw score that Cambridge deems worthy of an 'A'. These are not fixed percentages; they are calculated after all scripts have been marked to account for the specific difficulty of that year's papers. The primary purpose is to ensure that a student who gets a 'B' in 2026 has demonstrated the same level of economic understanding as a student who got a 'B' in any previous year.

## How 9708 Economics papers are marked
Your final A-Level Economics grade is built from your performance across several papers, each with a distinct marking style. The multiple-choice papers (Paper 1 for AS and Paper 3 for A2) are marked objectively, with one mark per correct answer. The data response and essay papers (Paper 2 for AS and Paper 4 for A2) use a combination of point-based marking for data interpretation and level-of-response marking for longer answers and essays. Essays are assessed against specific criteria for **Knowledge and Understanding**, **Application**, **Analysis**, and **Evaluation**, meaning the quality and structure of your argument are as important as the facts you include.

## Why grade boundaries change each session
Grade boundaries for 9708 Economics fluctuate from one session to the next for a simple reason: to maintain fairness and a consistent standard of achievement. No two exam papers are identical in difficulty. If senior examiners determine that a particular paper was slightly more challenging than the previous year's, the grade boundaries will be lowered to compensate. Conversely, if a paper is deemed more straightforward, the boundaries may be raised. This process ensures that your final grade reflects your economic ability, not the relative difficulty of the specific paper you happened to sit.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is it harder to get an A* in Economics now?
Cambridge is in the process of returning grade distributions to pre-pandemic levels, phasing out the more generous awarding approach used between 2020 and 2022. This means the percentage of students receiving top grades will align with 2019 statistics. It doesn't make the A* standard itself harder to achieve, but the statistical proportion of students awarded one is returning to the established norm.

### Can I just add up my component percentages to get my final grade?
No, this is a common mistake. Your final grade is determined by the sum of your weighted **raw marks** from all components, not by averaging percentages. This total raw score is then compared against the overall grade boundary for the qualification. Using percentages is inaccurate because it doesn't account for the different weightings of each paper or how the final threshold is set.

### If I perform poorly on one paper, can I still achieve a good overall grade?
Yes, it is possible. The A-Level is a compensatory qualification, meaning a very strong performance in one area can offset a weaker one elsewhere. For instance, excellent essay scores on Paper 4, where evaluation marks are crucial, can help compensate for a lower-than-expected score on the Paper 3 multiple-choice paper. Your final grade depends on your total weighted raw mark across all components.

## Bottom line
Instead of worrying about predicting the precise 2026 grade boundaries, concentrate on what you can control. Your focus should be on mastering economic concepts, practising exam technique with past papers, and deeply understanding the level-of-response marking criteria for essays. Consistent, high-quality preparation is the most effective way to ensure you achieve the grade you deserve, regardless of where the boundaries fall.

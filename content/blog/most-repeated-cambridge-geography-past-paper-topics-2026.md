Geography revision stabilises when you split **skills** (maps, graphs, fieldwork) from **themes** (population, hazards, development). This guide ranks what Cambridge returns to on **9696** (A-Level) and **0460** (IGCSE) from **2019—2025**.

> **Key takeaway:** Geography marks need **named case-study detail** — vague —a country in Africa— answers stay in Level 1.

## How this list was built

We weighted theme frequency on Paper 1-style questions, skills-paper mark schemes, examiner comments on weak evaluation, and case-study depth in level-of-response bands.

## A-Level Geography (9696)

| Priority | Theme / skill | Why it repeats |
|----------|---------------|----------------|
| 1 | **Population & migration** | Push/pull, policies, ageing populations |
| 2 | **Hydrology / water management** | Flooding, dams, sustainable supply |
| 3 | **Coastal / fluvial processes** | Landforms linked to named processes |
| 4 | **Atmosphere & climate change** | Causes, impacts, mitigation vs adaptation |
| 5 | **Urbanisation & settlement** | CBD models, squatter settlements, planning |
| 6 | **Hazardous environments** | Earthquake/volcano prep — human vs physical |
| 7 | **Global interdependence** | Trade, TNCs, development indicators |
| 8 | **Essay evaluation (AO3)** | —To what extent— with **weighed** judgement |

## Skills that repeat on every series

| Skill | Paper | Band trap |
|-------|-------|-----------|
| Map interpretation | 9696 / 0460 P2 | Describing without **explaining** pattern |
| Graph / statistical analysis | Both | No trend sentence or anomaly noted |
| Case-study essays | P1 themes | Generic place names |
| Fieldwork evaluation | 0460 P4 | Lists method without **validity/limitation** |

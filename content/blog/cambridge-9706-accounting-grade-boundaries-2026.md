The official grade boundaries for Cambridge International AS & A Level Accounting (9706) in 2026 will be published on results day, 13 August 2026. These boundaries, or 'grade thresholds', are the minimum raw marks you need to achieve a particular grade and are set after all exam papers have been marked. Understanding how these thresholds work is key to gauging your performance and managing expectations.

## What are grade boundaries?
A grade boundary is the minimum raw mark a candidate needs to secure a specific grade (from A* to E at A Level, or A to E at AS Level). Cambridge International sets these thresholds for each component paper and for the overall qualification option. For example, there will be a boundary for Paper 2, a separate one for Paper 3, and an overall boundary for the full A Level. This process ensures that a grade 'A' reflects the same standard of accounting knowledge and skill from one year to the next, regardless of minor variations in paper difficulty.

## How 9706 Accounting papers are marked
Your final raw mark in 9706 Accounting is built from marks awarded for specific points in your answers. The papers consist of structured questions requiring you to prepare financial statements, calculate and interpret ratios, or make business decisions based on accounting data. Marks are awarded for correct figures, accurate labels (e.g., 'non-current assets'), and correct accounting treatments. Crucially, for any calculation, you must show your **workings** clearly; method marks are often available even if your final figure is incorrect due to an earlier error. This cumulative raw score is what is ultimately measured against the grade boundaries.

## Why grade boundaries change each exam series
Grade boundaries are not fixed; they fluctuate between exam series (e.g., June 2025 vs. June 2026) for a very important reason: fairness. After marking, senior examiners analyse student performance and the overall difficulty of the papers. If a paper is deemed to be slightly more challenging than the previous year's, the grade boundaries will likely be lowered to compensate. Conversely, a more straightforward paper may have slightly higher boundaries. This system ensures that your grade reflects your underlying ability in accounting, not the luck of which specific paper you sat.

## Frequently asked questions

### If I get 80% of the raw marks, is that an A?
Not necessarily. Grades are not based on fixed percentages. An 'A' is awarded for reaching the specific raw mark threshold set for that paper in that session. This boundary could be higher or lower than 80% of the total marks, depending on the paper's difficulty and cohort performance.

### Are the grade boundaries for AS and A Level papers different?
Yes, absolutely. Each component has its own unique grade threshold. For example, the mark needed for an 'A' on Paper 2 (AS Level Financial Accounting) will be different from the mark needed for an 'A' on Paper 3 (A Level Financial Accounting). There are also separate boundaries for the overall AS and A Level qualifications.

### Does Cambridge use a 'curve' to set grades?
Cambridge uses a standards-referencing approach, not norm-referencing (or 'grading on a curve'). This means grades are not allocated to a fixed percentage of students. Instead, expert examiners determine the mark that represents a 'C' standard, for example, ensuring it reflects the same level of achievement as a 'C' in previous years.

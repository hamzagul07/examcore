Scoring highly in IB Chemistry HL is not about memorising more — it is about aligning your answers with what examiners reward in the **markbands**. Strategic use of [IB Chemistry past papers](/ib/past-papers/chemistry-hl) under timed conditions, honest self-marking, and targeted feedback closes the gap between a 5 and a 7.

## Understanding the IB Chemistry HL assessment
**Paper 1** MCQ. **Paper 2** structured and extended questions including AHL organic and physical chemistry. **Paper 3** data/option. **IA** individual investigation. Browse the full subject overview at [IB Chemistry HL](/ib/subjects/chemistry-hl).

## Markbands and what examiners reward
Long answers need balanced equations, state symbols, logical working, and **chemical reasoning** — not just a final number.

## A past paper workflow that actually works
Classify errors after each paper: calculation, mechanism, terminology, time. Drill one weak AHL topic before the next full paper.

## Paper-specific tips
Show working on every calculation. Organic mechanisms need unambiguous curly arrows.

## Common pitfalls
Unbalanced equations; missing units; skipping AHL organic practice; Data Booklet navigation under pressure.

## Using MarkScheme for targeted feedback
Self-marking against band descriptors is essential, but extended responses benefit from a second opinion. After a past paper or IA section, [get criterion-based feedback](/mark?subject=ib-chemistry-hl) aligned with IB assessment objectives — the same habits that lift exam scripts also sharpen coursework drafts.

Practise every paper with the booklet open — know where each section lives.

Use SL papers only for shared core topics at SL depth.

## Bottom line
Treat [IB Chemistry HL past papers](/ib/past-papers/chemistry-hl) as training for examiner thinking — not a trivia test. Cycle timed practice, honest band marking, and focused drills on your weakest criteria; that is how top candidates make a 7 predictable rather than lucky.

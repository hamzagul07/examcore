Scoring highly in IB Business Management SL is not about memorising more — it is about aligning your answers with what examiners reward in the **markbands**. Strategic use of [IB Business Management past papers](/ib/past-papers/business-management-sl) under timed conditions, honest self-marking, and targeted feedback closes the gap between a 5 and a 7.

## Understanding the IB Business Management SL assessment
**Paper 1** is based on a pre-seen case study (SL) with structured questions testing application of the business management toolkit. **Paper 2** uses an unseen stimulus with quantitative and qualitative prompts. Browse the full subject overview at [IB Business Management SL](/ib/subjects/business-management-sl).

## Markbands and what examiners reward
Top bands require **application to the case**, not generic textbook lists. Name the tool, apply it to the business in the stimulus, then evaluate limitations.

## A past paper workflow that actually works
Learn the toolkit by unit, then practise every past question by writing "tool → case evidence → so what → limitation" chains.

## Paper-specific tips
For Paper 1, annotate the case study when it is released — stakeholders, financials, CSR angles. For Paper 2, show calculations clearly with units (currency, ratios).

## Common pitfalls
Generic SWOT lists; no stakeholder named; forgetting to recommend a justified course of action; weak integration of HR/finance/marketing tools.

## Frequently asked questions

### When is the Paper 1 case released?
IBO releases it in advance of the session — your school will distribute it. Start annotating immediately.

### HL vs SL Paper 1 difference?
HL Paper 1 is an essay paper; SL is case-based structured questions — use SL-specific papers for timing.

### How to practise evaluation?
End answers with "however" — stakeholder conflict, short vs long term, or data limitations.

## Bottom line
Treat [IB Business Management SL past papers](/ib/past-papers/business-management-sl) as training for examiner thinking — not a trivia test. Cycle timed practice, honest band marking, and focused drills on your weakest criteria; that is how top candidates make a 7 predictable rather than lucky.

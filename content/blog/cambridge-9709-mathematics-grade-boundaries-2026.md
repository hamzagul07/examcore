The official grade boundaries for the Cambridge International 9709 Mathematics A-Level 2026 exams will be published on results day, 13 August 2026. These boundaries, or **grade thresholds**, are the minimum raw marks you need to achieve a specific grade (A, B, C, etc.) and are set after all marking is complete. While we cannot know the exact 2026 boundaries in advance, we can explain how they are determined and how you can make a reliable estimate of your performance.

## What are grade boundaries?
Grade boundaries are the link between your raw score on a paper and the grade you are awarded. For each component of the 9709 syllabus, such as Pure Mathematics 1 (9709/11) or Mechanics (9709/42), Cambridge sets a minimum number of marks required for each grade. For example, there will be a specific raw mark that is the lowest possible score to achieve an 'A', a 'B', and so on. These individual component thresholds are then combined to determine your overall qualification grade for AS or A-Level Mathematics.

## How Cambridge sets the 9709 grade boundaries
Contrary to a common myth, grade boundaries are not decided before the exam. They are established by senior examiners and statisticians *after* all the papers from an exam session have been marked. The primary goal is to maintain a consistent standard over time. If an exam paper is deemed more difficult than the previous year's, the grade boundaries will be lowered to compensate. Conversely, if a paper is found to be easier, the boundaries will be raised. This process ensures that your grade reflects your mathematical ability, not the relative difficulty of one particular exam paper.

## Understanding 9709 components and weighting
Cambridge 9709 Mathematics is a modular qualification built from several components. For an AS-Level, you will typically take Pure Mathematics 1 (P1) and either Mechanics (M1) or Probability & Statistics 1 (S1). For the full A-Level, you will add Pure Mathematics 3 (P3) and another applied paper (M1, S1, or S2). Each paper you sit contributes a specific number of raw marks, and each has its own grade boundary set after marking. These component scores are then weighted and combined to produce your final, overall grade. You can find detailed information on the structure and options on the main [9709 on MarkScheme](/subjects/9709) page.

## How to estimate your 2026 grade before results day
To form a realistic estimate of your grade, you must first practise and mark past papers. After completing a past paper under timed, exam-like conditions, the next step is to [mark a paper](/mark) meticulously using the official Cambridge mark scheme. Pay close attention to the allocation of method (M), accuracy (A), and independent (B) marks to get an accurate **raw mark**.

Once you have a raw mark for a paper, compare it against the published grade boundaries for that same paper from the two or three most recent exam sessions (e.g., June 2025, November 2025, and March 2026). This will give you a *range* of likely grades rather than a single, potentially misleading prediction. For example, if your raw mark of 55 on a P1 paper earned an 'A' in one session and a high 'B' in another, you know you are performing in the A/B border region. This method is the most reliable way to gauge your standing before the official results are released.

## Frequently asked questions

### When will the official 9709 June 2026 grade boundaries be released?

The official grade thresholds for the June 2026 exam series will be published on results day, which is Thursday, 13 August 2026. They are made available on the Cambridge International website at the same time that schools are able to release results to candidates, ensuring transparency for everyone involved.

### Is it true that grade boundaries are lower if the exam was hard?

Yes, absolutely. This is the fundamental principle of maintaining standards. Cambridge's expert panels analyse student performance data and the papers themselves. If a paper is judged to be more difficult than in previous years, the raw mark required for each grade will be lowered to ensure fairness across sessions.

### Where can I find past 9709 grade boundaries?

Cambridge publishes a 'Grade Threshold' document for every past exam series, which is available on their public website. These documents list the minimum raw marks needed for each grade in each component. You can also find these documents alongside past papers and other resources on dedicated subject pages.

### Does my P1 mark directly affect my M1 mark?

No, your performance on one paper does not directly influence your mark on another. Each component, whether it's P1, P3, M1, or S1, is marked independently and has its own raw score. Your overall qualification grade is calculated by combining the final, scaled marks from your required components according to syllabus rules.

## Bottom line
While it is useful to understand how grade boundaries work, you cannot control where they will be set for the 2026 exams. Your focus should be entirely on what you *can* control: maximising your raw marks. Concentrate on thorough revision, consistent past paper practice, and understanding the marking criteria. A strong performance in the exam hall will always result in a strong grade, regardless of how the boundaries shift.

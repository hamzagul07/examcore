## What are grade boundaries?

Grade boundaries are the minimum **raw marks** for each grade on every **0610** component you sat � MCQ, theory, and your practical or alternative-to-practical route. They are **not** fixed percentages: Cambridge publishes a separate threshold row per component code (for example `0610/21` vs `0610/22`). Your **overall** IGCSE grade uses a weighted aggregate; **A*** is decided on that total, not on one paper alone.

## How Cambridge sets 0610 boundaries

Thresholds are fixed **after** marking so comparable standards hold year to year. If a Paper 4 extended theory paper is harder than the previous June series, raw cut-offs usually **drop** � social media "everyone got a C" posts ignore this mechanism. Practical and ATP components have their own rows; never paste thresholds from a different paper variant ([wrong-session estimates](/insights)).

## What are grade boundaries?

Grade boundaries are the minimum **raw marks** for each grade on every **0620** component — MCQ, theory, and practical or ATP. Cambridge sets a separate threshold per component code; your **overall** grade is a weighted aggregate where **A*** reflects syllabus performance, not a single paper in isolation. Percentages from classroom tests do not translate directly to IGCSE raw marks.

## How Cambridge sets 0620 boundaries

Examiners finish marking before thresholds are published. Harder stoichiometry or structured papers usually mean **lower** raw cut-offs for the same grade — comparable outcomes, not "grade inflation." Check your exact paper numbers in the PDF; using last year's **5070** O-Level table for **0620** is a common results-week mistake ([insights](/insights)).

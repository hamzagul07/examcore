The best free revision resources for A-Level Maths (9709) are a strategic combination of official Cambridge materials and high-quality third-party websites. For a complete, effective, and entirely free workflow, you should start with official past papers, use Physics & Maths Tutor (PMT) for notes and topic questions, and then use MarkScheme to get your exam scripts marked accurately against the official criteria. This approach covers learning, practice, and feedback without costing you a penny.

## Start with the Source: Cambridge International
Your revision must be built on the foundation of official exam materials. Cambridge International provides free access to a trove of past papers, mark schemes, and examiner reports directly on their public website. These are the single most important resources because they are 100% authentic. Working through past papers under timed conditions simulates the real exam, while mark schemes show you exactly what examiners are looking for. Examiner reports are a hidden gem, offering detailed commentary on common student mistakes and areas of weakness from previous years, allowing you to avoid them yourself.

## Physics & Maths Tutor (PMT): The All-in-One Powerhouse
Physics & Maths Tutor, or PMT, is arguably the most comprehensive free resource available for Cambridge A-Level Maths. It hosts an incredible collection of materials specifically for the 9709 syllabus, including detailed revision notes for every module, from Pure Mathematics 1 to Statistics 2. More importantly, it offers a huge bank of past paper questions organised by topic. This allows you to focus your revision on specific areas, such as integration or vectors, and master them before tackling full papers. PMT also provides its own model worked solutions for many past papers, which are invaluable for understanding the methods required.

## MarkScheme: Free, Accurate Marking
Practising past papers is essential, but it's only half the battle. To truly improve, you need to know precisely where you went wrong. This is where [MarkScheme](/mark) provides a unique and crucial service. While you can self-assess with a mark scheme, it’s difficult to be objective and correctly apply the B1 (independent accuracy), M1 (method), and A1 (final accuracy) marks. MarkScheme allows you to upload your completed past paper script and receive free, expert marking that replicates the official Cambridge process. This feedback loop—practice, mark, review—is the fastest way to identify and fix weaknesses in your exam technique.

## Other Valuable Free Resources
While PMT is a giant, other sites also offer excellent free content. **Save My Exams** provides very clear, well-structured revision notes and topic questions that many students find helpful, though be aware that full access to all worked solutions and other features requires a paid subscription. For visual learners, YouTube is an indispensable tool. Channels like **TLMaths** and **ExamSolutions** offer thousands of free video tutorials covering specific concepts and walking through example problems in a clear, step-by-step manner. They are perfect for when you're stuck on a particular topic and need a teacher to explain it.

## Frequently asked questions

### How should I structure my revision using these free resources?
Start by using the notes from Physics & Maths Tutor to understand a topic. Solidify that knowledge by working through their topic-specific questions. Once you feel confident across several topics, attempt a full past paper from the Cambridge website under timed conditions. Finally, upload your work to [MarkScheme](/mark) for precise feedback.

### Are free resources enough to get an A* in A-Level Maths?
Absolutely. The combination of official Cambridge papers, detailed notes from sites like PMT, and precise feedback from a tool like MarkScheme provides everything a dedicated student needs. Consistency and mastering exam technique are the keys, not expensive subscriptions. These free tools are more than sufficient to reach the highest grades.

### Where can I find worked solutions for past papers?
Physics & Maths Tutor is the best place for this. They provide detailed, step-by-step worked solutions for a vast number of 9709 past papers. These are invaluable for understanding the methods required to solve complex problems and learning how to structure your answers to secure all available marks.

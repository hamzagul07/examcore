## Start with the Source: Cambridge International
Before you look anywhere else, your first stop must be the official Cambridge International website. Here you can find the holy trinity of exam preparation: **past papers**, **mark schemes**, and **examiner reports**, all available for free. These are the single most important resources because they are created by the people who set and mark your exams. Working through them ensures you understand the question style, the level of detail required, and the common pitfalls that trip up other students. Examiner reports, in particular, offer invaluable insight into what examiners were looking for in any given year.

## The Best Free Revision Notes: ZNotes
When it comes to learning and consolidating the syllabus content, ZNotes is the undisputed leader for free, high-quality revision notes. Created and curated by students who achieved A* grades, the notes are famous for being concise, syllabus-focused, and easy to digest. They cover a vast range of IGCSE and O-Level subjects, cutting through the textbook waffle to give you exactly what you need to know for the exam. They are perfect for building your initial understanding of a topic or for last-minute revision before tackling past papers.

## For STEM Subjects: Physics & Maths Tutor (PMT)
For students taking science, technology, engineering, and maths subjects, Physics & Maths Tutor (PMT) is an essential resource. While its name suggests a narrow focus, it offers an incredible depth of free material for Biology, Chemistry, Physics, Computer Science, and Maths. Its greatest strength lies in its extensive banks of **questions by topic**, which allow you to drill down on specific syllabus areas where you feel less confident. This targeted practice is perfect for bridging gaps in your knowledge after revising a topic using notes from a site like ZNotes.

## Frequently asked questions

### Are free resources good enough to get an A*?
Yes, absolutely. The quality of free resources like official past papers and ZNotes is exceptionally high. Success depends more on your consistent and effective use of these tools, rather than paying for expensive subscriptions. Diligent practice, timed paper attempts, and detailed analysis of your mistakes are the real keys to achieving a top grade.

### How many past papers should I do?
Quality over quantity is crucial. A good target is to complete the last 5–7 years of papers for each subject under timed conditions. More importantly, you should spend as much time reviewing your answers against the mark scheme and examiner reports as you did on the paper itself, ensuring you understand every mark gained and lost.

### What's the difference between IGCSE and O-Level resources?
While many topics overlap, IGCSE and O-Level are distinct qualifications with different syllabus codes and assessment styles. It is vital to ensure the resource you are using explicitly matches your syllabus (e.g., Cambridge IGCSE Physics 0625 vs. O-Level Physics 5054). All the reputable resources mentioned here clearly label their materials by qualification and syllabus code.

## Bottom line
You do not need to pay for expensive subscriptions to achieve the highest grades in your Cambridge exams. The most important ingredient is your own consistency and commitment to the process.

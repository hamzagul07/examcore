Securing an A* in Cambridge O-Level Mathematics 4024 is less about mathematical genius and more about disciplined execution. Top grades are built on a foundation of consistently earning full marks on questions you can do, while systematically eliminating the careless errors that erode your score. It requires a strategic approach to practice, a deep understanding of the mark scheme, and meticulous attention to detail on exam day.

## Plug Every Leak in Your Technique
At the A* level, the biggest threat isn't necessarily a difficult topic; it's the accumulation of small, avoidable mistakes. These include misreading the question, making a simple arithmetic slip under pressure, forgetting to include units, or not giving an answer to the required degree of accuracy. Treat these not as 'silly mistakes' but as technical flaws in your method. Actively hunt for them when you review your work and make it your primary mission to eliminate them. An A* is often the prize for the student who makes the fewest unforced errors.

## Show Every Step of Your Working
Examiners are not mind-readers; they can only award marks for what you write on the page. The 4024 marking system heavily rewards clear, logical working. You will see **method marks** (M marks) and **accuracy marks** (A marks) on every mark scheme. This means you can earn credit for demonstrating a correct method even if a subsequent slip leads to a wrong final answer. Conversely, a correct answer with no supporting working may score zero. Make a habit of laying out your solution step-by-step, as if you were explaining it to someone.

## Master Both Paper Formats
The non-calculator paper (Paper 1) and the calculator paper (Paper 2) test different but equally important skills. Success in Paper 1 demands fluency in mental and written arithmetic, a strong grasp of number properties, and the ability to work efficiently without technological aids. Paper 2 tests your ability to identify when and how to use your calculator effectively—for trigonometry, statistics, or complex calculations—and to interpret the results. Do not neglect one paper in favour of the other; your final grade depends on a strong performance across both.

## Leave No Syllabus Topic Behind
It is a dangerous strategy to hope that your weak topic won't appear on the exam. The Cambridge syllabus is a contract between you and the examiners, and they are entitled to set questions on any part of it. A* candidates have a solid understanding across the board. Use the official 4024 syllabus document as a checklist. As you revise, tick off each point, ensuring you can confidently tackle questions on everything from algebra and geometry to statistics and probability. This comprehensive knowledge is your best defence against being caught out by an unexpected question.

## Rehearse Your Performance with Timed Papers
The single most effective way to close the gap to an A* is to practise using [4024 past papers](/past-papers/4024) under strict, timed conditions. This isn't just about answering questions; it's about simulating the entire exam experience to build stamina and refine your time management. Once you've finished a paper, the real work begins. You must [mark a paper](/mark) yourself, being brutally honest and adhering strictly to the official mark scheme. This process reveals exactly where you are losing marks and why, allowing you to focus your revision with precision.

## Frequently asked questions

### How important is showing my working if I get the right answer?
It is absolutely critical. A significant portion of the marks for any given question are **method marks**, awarded for using a correct process. If you write down the correct answer with no working, the examiner may not be able to award you full credit. Showing your working is your safety net; it proves your understanding and can rescue marks even if you make a final calculation error.

### What's the best way to use my calculator in Paper 2?
Your calculator is a tool, not a replacement for thinking. Use it for complex arithmetic, trigonometric functions (sin, cos, tan), and statistical calculations. However, you must still show the steps in your method, such as writing down the formula you are using or the initial values you are substituting. The examiner needs to see your mathematical reasoning before the calculation.

### How many past papers should I complete?
Quality is far more important than quantity. It is better to complete 10 past papers, marking each one meticulously and spending time understanding every single mistake, than to rush through 30 papers without proper analysis. The goal is to learn from the process, identify patterns in your errors, and fix them. This detailed review is where the real improvement happens.

### I always run out of time. What can I do?
Poor time management is usually a symptom of another issue. Use timed past paper practice to diagnose the cause. Are you spending too long on questions you find difficult? Are you writing out your working too slowly? Or are you stuck on basic calculations in the non-calculator paper? Once you identify the bottleneck, you can target it with focused practice. For example, you can drill mental arithmetic or practise setting out standard question types more efficiently.

## Bottom line
An A* in Cambridge 4024 Mathematics is not awarded for flashes of brilliance, but for consistency, precision, and a robust exam technique. The path to this top grade involves mastering the entire syllabus, perfecting your working, and relentlessly practising under exam conditions to eliminate all avoidable errors. It is a grade earned through discipline and meticulous preparation.

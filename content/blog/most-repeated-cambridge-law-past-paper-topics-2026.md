Law revision stabilises when you separate **problem structure (IRAC)** from **essay authority**. Across Cambridge **9084** papers **2019–2025**, certain offences, torts and contract rules return regardless of scenario details. This guide ranks the patterns that move marks.

> **Key takeaway:** Problem marks come from **rules with cases/statutes applied to facts** — generic law notes without application score low.

## How this list was built

We weighted problem-question themes per paper, essay command-word frequency, and examiner comments on missing authority and unbalanced arguments.

## A-Level Law (9084)

| Priority | Paper / theme | Why it repeats |
|----------|---------------|----------------|
| 1 | **English Legal System (P1)** | Courts, ADR, precedent, legislation, judiciary |
| 2 | **Criminal — actus reus & mens rea (P2)** | Elements analysis on scenario facts |
| 3 | **Specific offences & defences (P2)** | Theft, assault, murder/manslaughter chains |
| 4 | **Contract formation & terms (P3)** | Offer, acceptance, consideration, vitiating factors |
| 5 | **Breach & remedies (P3)** | Damages types; equitable remedies where relevant |
| 6 | **Negligence (P4)** | Duty, breach, causation, remoteness — Donoghue line |
| 7 | **Other torts & defences (P4)** | Nuisance, vicarious liability, consent |
| 8 | **Essay balance & authority** | Named cases/statutes used to **test** the argument |

## IRAC habits that repeat

| Weak habit | Fix |
|------------|-----|
| Rule without authority | **Case name + principle** in one line |
| Facts copied into conclusion | Conclusion = **answer to the legal issue** |
| One-sided essay | Dedicated **counter** paragraph with rebuttal |
| Precedent lists | Use hierarchy — **which court binds whom** |

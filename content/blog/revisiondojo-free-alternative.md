## Quick answer

A free **RevisionDojo alternative** for IB: your school's official IB past papers and subject reports, free notes from IB communities, and **MarkScheme** ([markscheme.app/mark](/mark)) to mark your handwritten answers against IB assessment criteria plus [free IB courses](/ib/courses) for all seven groups. RevisionDojo is freemium — its coursework grader and full question bank are mostly paid.

RevisionDojo has become popular with IB students, largely on the back of its AI coursework grader for the Extended Essay, Internal Assessments and TOK, plus a large practice-question bank. It is a polished product, but you do not need a paid subscription to get high-quality, criteria-aligned feedback on your work. By combining the most authoritative IB materials with a free marking tool, you can build a revision system that is just as effective.

## Start with the source: official IB materials

Unlike some boards, the IB does not publish past papers freely online, so your first and most important port of call is your teacher or IB coordinator. Through your school you can access **past papers, mark schemes and subject reports** for your subjects. Subject reports are the hidden gem: they explain how candidates performed each session, the common mistakes that cost marks, and exactly what the top responses did. Building your revision around these documents — and the official assessment criteria for each subject — is the most direct way to align your work with what examiners reward.

## For practice questions

RevisionDojo's question bank is one of its main paid draws, but you can replicate targeted, topic-by-topic practice for free. Work through official past-paper questions grouped by syllabus topic, and use MarkScheme's [IB topic practice](/ib/past-papers) to pull real questions and mark your attempts. Keeping a simple log of paper, session and question number against each syllabus point gives you a customised question bank and deepens your familiarity with the real exams at the same time.

## For coursework and answer feedback

RevisionDojo is best known for its AI grader that scores an IA, EE or TOK essay against the rubric. MarkScheme takes a complementary approach for exam answers: you photograph a handwritten response and it marks it **against the IB assessment criteria and markbands**, criterion by criterion, showing where each mark is won or lost. It is honest about its limits — it is a fast second pass to train you to think like an examiner, not a guarantee of a grade, and you should always self-mark against the official criteria first. For understanding *why* a response sits in one markband rather than the next, engaging directly with the criteria on [our marking page](/mark) is the most effective habit you can build.

## For structured learning

RevisionDojo bundles notes and study tools; you can get a structured path for free. Your subject guide is the definitive checklist of everything you need to know, and MarkScheme offers [free IB courses](/ib/courses) across all seven subject groups that break each topic into manageable lessons. Combined with the [best free IB revision resources](/blog/best-free-ib-revision-resources-2026), this gives you a clear, no-cost structure for the whole two years.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is RevisionDojo worth the money?
RevisionDojo bundles an AI coursework grader, a large question bank and study tools into one convenient platform, which some students find worth the subscription. However, its core value — criteria-aligned practice and feedback — can be reproduced for free using official IB materials and a free marking tool, which are arguably more authoritative because they centre on the real assessment criteria and past papers.

### How is MarkScheme different from RevisionDojo's grader?
RevisionDojo focuses on grading coursework (IA, EE, TOK) against the rubric. MarkScheme is built for marking your *exam* answers — including long-answer and essay questions — against the IB assessment criteria and markbands, so you learn precisely how markbands are applied. MarkScheme does not mark against unofficial or reconstructed IB mark schemes; it marks against the published assessment criteria and is transparent about being a second pass rather than a final grade.

### Can I get IB feedback for free?
Yes. Self-mark against the official assessment criteria first, then use MarkScheme's [free marking](/mark) for a fast criterion-by-criterion second pass, and check your reasoning against the subject report for that session. This mirrors the feedback loop a paid grader offers, at no cost.

## Bottom line

RevisionDojo is a slick product, but it is not essential for a strong IB result. A focused student can build an equally effective, criteria-driven revision system for free by combining official IB past papers and subject reports, community notes, and the self-marking tools and free courses on MarkScheme. Top grades come from deeply understanding the assessment criteria — and the free, official route is the most direct way there.

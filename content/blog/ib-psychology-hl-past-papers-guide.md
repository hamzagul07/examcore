Scoring highly in IB Psychology HL is not about memorising more — it is about aligning your answers with what examiners reward in the **markbands**. Strategic use of [IB Psychology past papers](/ib/past-papers/psychology-hl) under timed conditions, honest self-marking, and targeted feedback closes the gap between a 5 and a 7.

## Understanding the IB Psychology HL assessment
**Paper 1** biological, cognitive, sociocultural approaches. **Paper 2** two options. **Paper 3** (HL) qualitative research methods and analysis. Browse the full subject overview at [IB Psychology HL](/ib/subjects/psychology-hl).

## Markbands and what examiners reward
Essays need **named studies** with method, findings, and explicit link to the question.

## A past paper workflow that actually works
Study sheets per study; timed SAQs; essay plan with two studies + counter + conclusion. Paper 3: practise qualitative analysis prompts.

## Paper-specific tips
Match command terms — discuss needs balance; evaluate needs judgement.

## Common pitfalls
Vague studies; neglecting Paper 3; confusing HL qualitative content with SL-only prep.

## Using MarkScheme for targeted feedback
Self-marking against band descriptors is essential, but extended responses benefit from a second opinion. ## Frequently asked questions

### How many studies per essay?
Two developed studies plus evaluation usually beats four shallow mentions.

### Paper 3 for SL?
Not examined at SL — HL must practise Paper 3 separately.

### Courses?
[Psychology HL](/ib/courses/psychology-hl) and [SL](/ib/courses/psychology-sl).

## Bottom line
Treat [IB Psychology HL past papers](/ib/past-papers/psychology-hl) as training for examiner thinking — not a trivia test. Cycle timed practice, honest band marking, and focused drills on your weakest criteria; that is how top candidates make a 7 predictable rather than lucky.

In May 2026, Cambridge International confirmed that **several live papers were leaked** before or during the exam window. The board's response — **voiding affected components** and awarding **assessed marks** instead — is now one of the most searched questions on student forums, WhatsApp groups, and Reddit threads worldwide.

If your physics, maths, or computer science component was impacted, you are not alone — and you are not powerless. Here is how assessed marks actually work, what Cambridge has announced, and how to protect the rest of your grade.

## Quick answer

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| What is an assessed mark? | A **calculated** mark for a component you did not sit (or whose paper was voided), based on your performance on **other components** in the same syllabus |
| Do assessed marks hurt my grade? | Cambridge says the method places you in the **same relative position** as on the papers you did sit — no systematic advantage or disadvantage |
| Will results still come on 11 August 2026? | **Yes** — Cambridge has confirmed the June 2026 results day is unchanged |
| Should I stop revising other papers? | **No** — every remaining component still counts fully; strong marks there strengthen your assessed-mark profile |

> **Key takeaway:** Assessed marks are Cambridge's standard fairness tool when a component cannot be used. They are not a punishment — but they also are not something you can game. Focus on the papers you **will** sit.

## How Cambridge calculates assessed marks

When a component cannot be used to award a grade, Cambridge does **not** leave a hole in your certificate. Instead, senior assessment experts apply a **well-established statistical method**:

1. Take the marks you earned on the **other components** you sat in that syllabus entry
2. Compare your performance on those components to **all candidates worldwide** who sat them
3. Place you in the **same relative position** for the missing/voided component as you achieved on the rest

### A simple illustration

Imagine a syllabus where Paper 1, Paper 2, and Paper 3 each count 33%. You sat Paper 1 and Paper 3 strongly — around the **75th percentile** globally. Paper 2 was voided. Cambridge awards an assessed mark for Paper 2 that also places you near the **75th percentile** for that component, based on the global cohort.

This is why Cambridge states that candidates with assessed marks should have **no systematic advantage or disadvantage** compared with students who sat every paper normally.

## Replacement exams vs assessed marks

Cambridge uses **two different tools** depending on timing and security:

| Situation | Typical response |
|-----------|------------------|
| Paper leaked **before** the sitting (candidates had not yet taken it) | **New paper** sat on a rescheduled date |
| Paper sat but later voided due to breach | **Assessed mark** for that component |
| Paper postponed pending investigation | **Replacement sitting** announced to schools |

## What you should do right now

### 1. Check official school communications only

Cambridge briefs **exams officers**, not TikTok. Your centre will tell you:

- Whether you are on **assessed marks**, a **replacement paper**, or unaffected
- Venue, timing, and entry/withdrawal deadlines

### 2. Maximise marks on every remaining component

Assessed marks pull from your **other** papers. A strong Paper 4 or coursework component directly supports a fair calculated mark elsewhere.

| Action | Why |
|--------|-----|
| Timed past paper on remaining components | Builds exam fitness |
| Strict mark-scheme marking | Finds repeatable errors |
| Examiner report for your syllabus code | Shows what lost marks that series |

Use [9709 past papers](/past-papers/9709) or your subject hub — then [mark your answers](/mark) against the real scheme.

### 3. Do not chase "replacement paper leaks"

Replacement sittings use **new confidential papers**. Any file claiming to be the June 8 or June 9 paper is either fake or malpractice. Report it to your exams officer.

### 4. Document your own integrity

If group chats shared suspicious material:

- Do not forward or save files
- Tell your exams officer **when** you received content and that you did not use it
- Keep revising with **published** past papers only

## Assessed marks and university offers

Cambridge has stated that June 2026 grades will release **on schedule (11 August 2026)** so university offer deadlines are met. Assessed marks appear on your results statement like any other component mark — universities are familiar with this process from prior breach years.

If you are applying through UCAS or international equivalents, your centre can confirm how Cambridge reports voided components on statements of results.

## How this differs from IB grade boundaries

Cambridge **assessed marks** solve a **missing component** problem after a security breach. IB **grade boundaries** solve a **difficulty calibration** problem after marking — converting raw marks to 1–7. Different systems, different triggers. ## FAQ

### Will I know I got an assessed mark before results day?

Your exams officer may know which components are affected before results release. The final certificate shows component marks without always labelling them "assessed" to students — ask your centre if you need confirmation for university forms.

### Can I appeal an assessed mark?

Standard enquiry about results processes apply. Grounds usually require evidence of an **administrative error**, not disagreement with the statistical method itself.

### What if I missed another component through illness?

Assessed marks for leaks are different from **special consideration**. Illness exemptions follow Cambridge's separate procedures — speak to your exams officer immediately.

### Does this affect O-Level and IGCSE students?

The May 2026 news cycle focused on **international A-Level and AS-Level** breaches in specific zones. IGCSE integrity rules are the same in principle — your school communicates any centre-specific actions.

The grade boundaries for the Cambridge 9609 Business A-Level in 2026 will be released on results day, which for the June 2026 exam session is 13 August 2026. These boundaries, or **grade thresholds**, are the minimum raw marks you need to achieve a specific grade (A, B, C, etc.) and are set *after* all papers have been marked. They are not predetermined, so it is impossible to know the exact 2026 boundaries in advance; however, by understanding how they work, you can effectively estimate your performance.

## What are grade boundaries?
Grade boundaries are the minimum number of raw marks a candidate needs to secure a particular grade for a component (a paper) and for the overall qualification. They are not percentages. For example, the boundary for a grade 'A' is the lowest raw mark that is judged to be of 'A' standard. Cambridge International sets these thresholds for every exam session to ensure that it is no more or less difficult to get a grade in one session than in another.

## How Cambridge sets grade boundaries for 9609 Business
Cambridge's primary goal when setting grade boundaries is to maintain a consistent standard over time. The process involves a panel of expert examiners who review student work from the current session. They compare this work to scripts from previous years to determine the level of performance that represents a grade 'A', 'B', and so on. If a paper is deemed slightly more difficult than in the previous year, the grade boundaries may be lowered to compensate, ensuring that students are not disadvantaged. Conversely, if a paper is found to be easier, the boundaries might be raised.

## The role of marking in 9609 Business
Understanding the marking style for 9609 Business is crucial, as it directly influences the raw mark you achieve. Both the essay and case-study papers are marked using a **level-of-response** system. This means you are not just awarded marks for stating correct facts, but for demonstrating higher-order skills. Examiners look for your ability to apply concepts to the given business scenario (**application**), develop arguments with clear cause-and-effect reasoning (**analysis**), and make well-supported judgements (**evaluation**). The quality of your answers determines your level, and therefore your mark, which is then measured against the grade boundary. ## Why grade boundaries change between exam sessions
Grade boundaries for 9609 Business fluctuate between different exam sessions (e.g., June 2025 vs November 2025) for two main reasons: variations in paper difficulty and the overall performance of the student cohort. Even with rigorous quality control, the challenge level of an exam paper can vary slightly from one year to the next. By adjusting the grade boundaries, Cambridge ensures that a student's final grade reflects their subject ability, not their luck in sitting a marginally easier or harder paper. This process guarantees that an 'A' in 2026 represents the same level of achievement as an 'A' in any other year.

## Frequently asked questions

### Will the 2026 grade boundaries be higher or lower than in 2025?
It is impossible to predict whether the 2026 boundaries will be higher or lower. This depends entirely on the perceived difficulty of the 2026 exam papers and the overall performance of the candidates. Cambridge's objective is to maintain a consistent standard, so the boundaries will be adjusted to reflect that.

### Where can I find the official 9609 grade boundaries?
Cambridge publishes the official grade threshold tables on their public website on results day (13 August 2026 for the June series). ### Is an A always 80%?
No, this is a common myth. A-Level grades are not based on fixed percentages. The raw mark needed for a grade 'A' is determined by the grade boundary set for that specific exam session. This figure is often different from 80% of the total marks and changes each year to ensure fairness.

### How does the weighting factor affect my overall grade?
Each paper (component) you sit is weighted to ensure it contributes the correct proportion to your final A-Level or AS Level grade. Cambridge applies a weighting factor to your raw component score before adding them together to get your total weighted mark.

The official grade boundaries for the Cambridge International A-Level Islamic Studies (9488) 2026 exam series will be published on results day, which for the June 2026 session is 13 August 2026. These boundaries, also known as **grade thresholds**, are the minimum raw marks you need to achieve for each grade (A*, A, B, etc.) and are only determined *after* all exam papers have been marked. This ensures that standards are consistent year on year, regardless of minor variations in paper difficulty.

## What are grade boundaries?
A grade boundary is the lowest raw mark a candidate can get on a paper, or for the overall qualification, and still be awarded a particular grade. For 9488 Islamic Studies, Cambridge sets separate thresholds for each component paper as well as an overall threshold for the final A-Level grade. It is crucial to understand that these are not percentages; they are absolute marks out of the total available for that paper. For example, the boundary for a grade 'A' is the specific mark that Cambridge's senior examiners agree represents an 'A' standard performance for that particular exam session.

## How Cambridge sets the 9488 boundaries
The process of setting grade boundaries is a rigorous one designed to ensure fairness. Cambridge does not decide in advance that a certain percentage will equal a certain grade. Instead, after all marking is complete, a panel of senior examiners analyses student scripts at a statistical and qualitative level. They review the quality of work at different mark points and use their expert judgement, alongside statistical data, to determine the mark that reflects the established standard for a grade A, B, C, and so on. This means if a paper proves to be more challenging than in previous years, the grade boundaries may be slightly lower; conversely, if a paper is more straightforward, they may be slightly higher.

## Connecting boundaries to 9488 assessment
Achieving a high grade in 9488 Islamic Studies is about more than just recalling information; it's about demonstrating higher-order skills. The papers assess your textual knowledge of the Qur'an and Hadith, your understanding of key themes, and your ability to construct well-supported evaluative essays. Marking is based on a **level-of-response** (LoR) model, which rewards the quality of your analysis and argumentation. To reach the marks required for the higher grade boundaries, you must show an ability to explain concepts clearly, use source references accurately, and present balanced, thoughtful evaluations of different scholarly viewpoints. Simply listing facts will not be sufficient to secure top grades.

## Frequently asked questions

### Why do grade boundaries change every year?
Grade boundaries change to ensure fairness and maintain standards across different exam sessions. Minor variations in the difficulty of an exam paper are inevitable. By adjusting the boundaries up or down after marking, Cambridge ensures that a student's final grade reflects their subject ability, not their luck in sitting a slightly easier or harder paper.

### Is there a formula to predict the 2026 boundaries?
No, there is no mathematical formula to predict future grade boundaries. The final thresholds are set using a combination of statistical analysis and the professional judgement of senior examiners after marking is complete. The only reliable method for estimation is to compare your practice paper scores against data from recent, published exam series.

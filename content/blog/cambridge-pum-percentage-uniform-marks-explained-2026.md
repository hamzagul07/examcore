Parents see **B (72)** on a statement and ask: “Is that almost an A?” The number in brackets is usually **PUM** — percentage uniform mark — and it answers a different question than your last past-paper percentage. Here is how PUM works for Cambridge **2026** results and why it matters for resits.

## Quick answer

| Term | What it is |
|------|------------|
| **Raw mark** | Points you earned on the paper before grade boundaries |
| **Grade** | A*, A, B… set after the exam series |
| **PUM** | A **0–100 scaled score** showing where you sit **inside** your grade band |

Cambridge uses PUM on many international statements. Some school portals label it differently — the idea is the same: **fine grain within a grade**, not a second grade.

> **Key takeaway:** PUM 79 as a B is strong. PUM 61 as a B is fragile. Same letter, different story.

Want your own number? Use the [PUM / UMS calculator](/tools/pum-calculator) to convert a raw mark to a Percentage Uniform Mark on the 0–100 scale.

## PUM grade bands (typical Cambridge international)

| Grade | PUM range (typical) |
|-------|---------------------|
| A* | 90–100 |
| A | 80–89 |
| B | 70–79 |
| C | 60–69 |
| D | 50–59 |
| E | 40–49 |

Exact reporting varies by qualification and zone — always read the legend on **your** PDF statement.

## PUM is not your past-paper percentage

When you self-mark a 2023 paper at home:

- You may award **method marks** generously  
- Essay bands drift at midnight  
- You ignore **standardisation** across the cohort  

PUM is set **after** Cambridge applies grade boundaries for the **real series**. Your 78% on a 2022 paper does not automatically mean PUM 78 on results day.

Bridge the gap with [grade boundaries guide](/blog/cambridge-grade-boundaries-past-papers) and honest marking.

## Raw marks vs PUM — worked example (conceptual)

Suppose a component is marked out of 75:

1. You score **52 raw**  
2. Cambridge sets the **B threshold** at 48 raw for that series  
3. You receive grade **B**  
4. PUM might be **74** — high B, close to A boundary on combined components  

Another student scores **49 raw** — also B, but PUM **71**. Same grade on certificate; different resit urgency.

## How to use PUM after results day

| PUM pattern | Suggested action |
|-------------|------------------|
| Grade met, PUM high in band | Maintain skills; do not cram resit |
| Grade met, PUM low in band | Fix weak components before A2 / university |
| Grade missed by one band, PUM 78+ | Discuss [EAR](/blog/cambridge-enquiry-about-results-ear-guide-2026) + November resit |
| Grade missed, PUM mid-band | Resit with new marking workflow — not remark alone |

## PUM vs UMS (Edexcel) — do not mix boards

**UMS** is a Pearson Edexcel concept for modular GCSE/A-Level. **Cambridge International does not use UMS** on the same model. If your school runs both boards, compare like with like — Cambridge **PUM** only on Cambridge statements.

## Improving PUM on a resit

PUM rises when **raw marks** rise across components. The fastest raw-mark gains come from:

1. **Mark schemes** — lose fewer “one-word” definition marks  
2. **Timing** — finish every question (blank = zero)  
3. **Command words** — [explain vs evaluate](/blog/cambridge-command-words-past-papers-guide)  
4. **Marked past papers weekly** — upload to [MarkScheme](/mark)

## FAQ

### Does Cambridge publish PUM boundaries in advance?

No — grade thresholds publish **after** each series. Use past thresholds as context only.

### Will universities see PUM?

Some receive full statements; many see grades only. Check application portals.

### Is PUM on O-Level and IGCSE?

Many international statements include PUM or equivalent scaled information — format depends on zone.

## Bottom line

PUM tells you **how safe** your grade is and **how close** you are to the next band. Use it to choose between remark, resit, and move-on — then back every decision with **marked past papers**, not vibes.

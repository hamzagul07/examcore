Cambridge **9709 Mathematics** is almost entirely **point-marked** — command words tell you how much working to show, not how eloquent to be. Misreading **Calculate** as “write the answer only” or skipping steps on **Show that** costs method marks every series.

## Quick answer

| Verb | What 9709 examiners want |
|------|--------------------------|
| **Calculate / Find / Solve** | Formula → substitution → answer with unit/s.f. — **working required** |
| **Show that** | Prove the given result — **no skipped algebra** |
| **Hence** | Use the previous part — must reference it explicitly |
| **Write down** | Often one mark — no working needed unless stated |

> **Key takeaway:** Method marks (M1) rescue wrong finals when working is correct. The command word tells you how many lines to write.

## Calculate, Find, and Solve

These verbs demand **visible method**:

1. State the formula or approach
2. Substitute values with units
3. Give the final answer to appropriate **significant figures**

Examiners apply [B1/M1/A1 rules](/blog/cambridge-a-level-maths-mark-scheme-b1-m1-a1). A correct number with no working often scores **zero** if the method was wrong.

## Show that and Hence

**Show that** gives you the answer — your job is a **complete logical chain**. Skipping “obvious” steps loses marks because the examiner cannot see your reasoning.

**Hence** links parts — start with “From part (a)…” or equivalent. Using a fresh method when **Hence** was required may score limited credit.

## Write down and State

Low-tariff recall — one line is enough. Do not waste time proving a **Write down** identity.

## FAQ

### Does Evaluate appear in 9709?
Rarely on maths papers — judgement verbs belong to humanities. If it appears, treat it as “justify with maths.”

### What about proof questions?
**Prove** and **Show that** need rigorous logic — every implication must follow.

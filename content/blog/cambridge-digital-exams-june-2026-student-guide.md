Cambridge is rolling out **digital examinations** from the **June 2026** series. Most students worldwide will still sit **paper** exams — but if your school joins the Early Adopter Programme, some of your papers may move to a **laptop under exam conditions**. Here is what is actually changing, what is not, and how to prepare without panic.

## Quick answer

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| Are all Cambridge exams digital in 2026? | **No** — only selected papers in selected regions |
| Is the syllabus different? | **No** — same content, same mark schemes |
| Can grades go down because it is digital? | Cambridge says digital papers meet the same standards as paper |
| What should you practise? | **Past papers** — timing, mark schemes, and typed answers where relevant |

> **Key takeaway:** Digital delivery changes **how** you write, not **what** examiners reward. Mark schemes still decide your grade.

## Which exams go digital first (June 2026)

Cambridge’s first phase targets schools in **Europe, the Middle East, North Africa, and the US** that opt into the Early Adopter Programme. Papers announced for this phase include:

| Qualification | Syllabus | Papers (initial digital phase) |
|---------------|----------|--------------------------------|
| Cambridge IGCSE Accounting | 0452 | Paper 01 (MCQ) |
| Cambridge IGCSE Economics | 0455 | Paper 01 (MCQ) |
| Cambridge IGCSE Biology | 0610 | Papers 01, 02 (MCQ) |
| Cambridge IGCSE Chemistry | 0620 | Papers 01, 02 (MCQ) |
| Cambridge IGCSE Physics | 0625 | Papers 01, 02 (MCQ) |
| Cambridge International AS Level | 8021 English General Paper | Papers 01, 02 |

**Paper-based exams continue alongside digital** — your centre chooses which mode you enter. By 2030 Cambridge aims for most exams to have a digital option; by 2033 roughly **85%** of qualifications may offer one. That is a long rollout — 2026 is the start, not the finish.

Sources: [Cambridge digital examinations overview](https://www.cambridgeinternational.org/programmes-and-qualifications/developing-digital-examinations/), [June 2026 launch announcement](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20240930936487/en/Cambridge-International-Education-to-launch-digital-examinations-in-June-2026).

## What stays the same

Cambridge describes the 2026 move as **migratory** — existing papers transferred to screens with minimal change:

- **Syllabus content** — unchanged  
- **Assessment objectives** — unchanged  
- **Mark schemes** — same marking points (MCQ keys stay binary)  
- **Exam conditions** — centre invigilation, no internet, no AI tools  

Structured and essay papers are **not** in the first wave for most subjects. If you are doing 9709 calculus or 9708 essays in June 2026, you are almost certainly still on paper unless your centre tells you otherwise.

## What feels different on a laptop

Even when content is identical, the **experience** changes:

| Paper habit | Digital habit to build |
|-------------|------------------------|
| Circling key words in the stem | Highlight tool or underline on screen |
| Rough working in margins | Blank paper allowed at centre — confirm with exams officer |
| MCQ bubbling | Click selection — mis-clicks happen; double-check before submit |
| Longer written answers | Typing speed and paragraph editing under time pressure |

Schools in the Early Adopter Programme run **familiarisation sessions** and digital mocks. Treat those like real past papers — sit timed, then **mark against the official scheme**.

## How to revise for digital MCQ papers

MCQ digital papers (0610/0620/0625/0452/0455 Paper 01) reward the same skills as paper:

1. **Topic drills** from past MCQ sessions — see [MCQ marking guide](/blog/cambridge-mcq-past-papers-how-to-mark)  
2. **Wrong-option diary** — why each distractor tempted you  
3. **Timed full Paper 01** — 45 minutes, no pauses  
4. **Mark strictly** — no “I knew B” partial credit  

For sciences, high-yield MCQ topics overlap with structured papers: electrolysis, mole calculations, electricity, forces. Cross-check [IGCSE most-tested topics](/blog/cambridge-igcse-most-tested-past-paper-topics-2026).

## English General Paper (8021) on screen

If your centre offers digital **AS English General Paper**, you will type essays and shorter responses. Past-paper practice should include:

- **Planning on paper first** — 5 minutes before typing (see [essay planning guide](/blog/a-level-essay-planning-past-papers))  
- **Word-count discipline** — on-screen counters tempt over-writing  
- **Mark scheme bands** — argument structure beats fluent typing  

Upload practice paragraphs to [MarkScheme](/mark) if you do not have a teacher marking weekly.

## What if your school is not in the programme

Most Cambridge schools worldwide will see **no change** in June 2026. Do not buy a laptop “for exams” unless your exams officer confirms digital entry. Your revision plan stays:

- Official past papers + mark schemes  
- [Self-marking workflow](/blog/how-to-mark-cambridge-past-papers-yourself)  
- [May/June countdown](/blog/cambridge-may-june-2026-exam-series-countdown) if you are sitting this series  

## Digital exams and integrity

In the exam room: **no ChatGPT, no messaging, no second device**. Centre rules apply exactly as for paper. For revision at home, see [AI rules for Cambridge](/blog/chatgpt-and-ai-cambridge-exams-2026-rules) — AI explains; it does not sit the paper for you.

## FAQ

### Will Pakistan / India / Southeast Asia get digital exams in 2026?

The announced June 2026 phase focuses on Europe, Middle East, North Africa, and the US. Other regions follow in later rollout waves — ask your centre, do not assume from social media.

### Do I need to practise typing instead of handwriting?

Only for components your centre confirms as digital. Otherwise keep handwriting practice for structured papers — especially maths working and diagrams.

### Are digital MCQ papers easier?

No — same questions, same difficulty. Some students lose marks to **mis-clicks** or rushing the on-screen review step. Build a “final pass” habit: scroll every question before submit.

### Where can I mark practice answers?

[MarkScheme](/mark) marks typed or photographed handwritten answers against Cambridge mark schemes — useful whether your final exam is paper or digital.

## Bottom line

June 2026 digital exams are a **pilot for some MCQ and AS GP papers**, not a whole-curriculum switch. Revise the same syllabus, mark the same schemes, and use familiarisation mocks if your school offers them. The students who gain an edge will be those who **practise under timed conditions and mark honestly** — not those who chase rumoured “easier” formats online.

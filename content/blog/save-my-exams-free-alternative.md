## Quick answer

A free **Save My Exams alternative** for Cambridge: official past papers + mark schemes (Cambridge International), notes from **Physics & Maths Tutor** or **ZNotes**, and **MarkScheme** ([markscheme.app/mark](/mark)) to mark handwritten answers against the real scheme plus [free courses](/courses). Save My Exams is freemium — most detailed content is paid.

While paid platforms like Save My Exams offer a convenient, all-in-one solution for Cambridge revision, they are not the only path to a top grade. By combining a few powerful and completely free resources, you can build a revision toolkit that is just as effective, if not more so, because it centres on the most authoritative materials available: those from Cambridge International itself.

## Start with the Source: Official Cambridge Resources
Before looking anywhere else, your first port of call must be the official Cambridge International website. Here you can find a treasure trove of **past exam papers, mark schemes, and examiner reports**—all for free. These are the single most important resources for your revision because they are the literal source of truth. Past papers show you the exact format and style of questions you will face, while mark schemes reveal precisely what examiners are instructed to reward. Examiner reports are the hidden gem, providing invaluable commentary on how students performed in previous years, highlighting common mistakes to avoid and showcasing what top-performing candidates did right. Building your revision around these documents is the most direct way to align your work with the examiner's expectations.

## For High-Quality Notes: PMT and ZNotes
Save My Exams is well-known for its detailed, syllabus-specific revision notes, but excellent free alternatives exist that cover the same ground. **Physics & Maths Tutor (PMT)** has expanded far beyond its original subjects and now offers comprehensive notes, definitions, and summaries for a huge range of IGCSE and A-Level subjects. These notes are detailed, reliable, and closely follow the syllabus structure. For a more condensed approach, **ZNotes** provides concise, community-vetted revision notes that are perfect for quick topic reviews or last-minute consolidation. Using a combination of PMT for in-depth understanding and ZNotes for high-level summaries gives you a powerful, flexible, and free alternative to paid notes.

## For Topic Questions and Practice
One of the most useful features of paid platforms is their bank of questions sorted by topic, which is great for targeted revision. You can replicate this for free with a little organisation. Resources like PMT offer their own free topic-based question banks, compiled from past papers. Alternatively, you can create your own. Simply download a batch of past papers and, with your syllabus in hand, go through them and identify which questions correspond to which topic. Keep a simple document or spreadsheet listing the paper, year, and question number next to each syllabus point. This process not only gives you a customised question bank but also deepens your familiarity with the exam papers themselves.

## For Accurate Marking and Feedback
After attempting past paper questions, getting accurate feedback is crucial. While Save My Exams offers its 'Smart Mark' AI for some short-answer questions, the ultimate way to understand the marking process is to use the official mark scheme yourself. This is where MarkScheme comes in. Our free tool allows you to pull up any official Cambridge past paper and mark your own work against the official scheme, line by line, in a clean, digital interface. This process does more than just give you a score; it trains you to think like an examiner, internalise the key terminology required for marks, and understand exactly how points are awarded for every part of a question. For truly effective self-assessment that prepares you for the real exam, there is no substitute for engaging directly with the mark scheme, which you can do easily on our [free marking page](/mark).

## Frequently asked questions

### Is Save My Exams worth the money?
Save My Exams offers significant convenience by bundling high-quality notes, topic questions, and AI marking tools into a single, easy-to-navigate platform. For students who value this streamlined experience and have the budget, it can be a worthwhile investment. However, all of its core functions can be replicated for free using the resources mentioned above, which are arguably more authoritative as they centre on official exam materials.

### How is MarkScheme different from Save My Exams' AI marker?
Save My Exams' 'Smart Mark' uses AI to automatically grade certain short-answer questions, providing instant feedback. MarkScheme, on the other hand, is a tool designed for you to manually and meticulously mark your *entire* paper—including long-answer and essay questions—against the *official* Cambridge mark scheme. Our tool facilitates a deeper learning process, helping you internalise the precise criteria and keywords examiners use, a skill AI cannot fully teach.

## Bottom line
While Save My Exams is a polished and convenient product, it is by no means essential for achieving top grades. A focused student can build an equally, if not more, effective revision system for free by strategically combining official Cambridge past papers, notes from PMT or ZNotes, and the self-marking tools on MarkScheme.

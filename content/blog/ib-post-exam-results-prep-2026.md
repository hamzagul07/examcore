The weeks **after** your last IB paper are either wasted on rumour threads or used to build a **results-day file** your coordinator can act on. May 2026 results land in **early July** — this checklist turns waiting time into evidence.

## Quick answer

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| When are May 2026 IB results? | **Early July 2026** — often **6 July** (confirm with your IB Coordinator) |
| Where do I log in? | **candidates.ibo.org** — personal code + PIN from school |
| Can I predict my grade now? | **Estimate** from marked mocks and IA feedback — not from Reddit |
| Should I revise for November already? | Light **technique** review only; rest first |
| What to save? | Marked past papers, IA drafts, coordinator contact |
| What to ignore? | Fake boundary spreadsheets, "leaked" grade lists |

> **Key takeaway:** Build a file your **IB Coordinator** can use for EUR, retakes, or university calls if grades surprise you.

## Week 1 after exams — decompress with structure

| Day | Action |
|-----|--------|
| 1–2 | Sleep; no post-mortem marathons |
| 3 | One page per subject: "felt strong / weak / ran out of time" |
| 4–5 | Pull **marked** past papers from the last month — highlight band patterns |
| 6–7 | List university conditions (points needed per subject) |

Avoid posting specific questions online — malpractice investigations use social trails.

## Build your honest grade estimate

You are not predicting boundaries; you are predicting **your performance band**.

1. **IA grades** — note what your school submitted (if disclosed).  
2. **Exam papers** — compare to marked mocks on the same command words.  
3. **Core** — TOK/EE predicted grades from supervisors (if shared).  
4. **Total bracket** — subject points + realistic Core bonus (0–3).

Tool: [criterion marking](/mark) on one weak script per subject — band descriptors beat gut feel.

Boundary context: [IB grade boundaries explained](/blog/ib-grade-boundaries-explained) ? [grade 7 buffer strategy](/blog/ib-how-to-build-a-grade-7-buffer-2026).

## Save this evidence file

| Item | Why |
|------|-----|
| PDF/screenshots of marked mocks | EUR and retake targeting |
| IA feedback sheets | Shows where exam marks must compensate |
| Candidate login details (offline) | Avoid portal panic on results day |
| IB Coordinator email/phone | EUR and retake registration |
| University admissions numbers | Narrow misses need a same-day call |

## EUR and retakes — decide criteria now

**Enquiry Upon Results (EUR)** can raise or **lower** grades. Before paying:

- Is the gap **one band** on a high-stakes subject?  
- Do markbands show you **just missed** the level above?  
- Can university still accept you **without** EUR?

Read: [predicted grades & EUR](/blog/ib-predicted-grades-explained). November retakes: [IB exam revision strategy](/blog/ib-exam-revision-strategy).

| Hour | Action |
|------|--------|

## Cambridge students in the same window

Cambridge June results release **11 August** (A-Level) and **18 August** (IGCSE/O Level) — different calendar. See [Cambridge post-exam prep](/blog/cambridge-post-exam-results-prep-2026).

## Frequently asked questions

### Should I keep studying?

Light review is fine; full cram is not. Rest restores performance for November retakes.

### How accurate are online IB boundary predictors?

Treat them as entertainment. Official tables publish **with** results in July.

### When do universities get my grades?

Your school transmits them; timing varies. Have PDFs ready for clearing if needed.

### Is AI useful while waiting?

For **explaining** errors in marked papers — yes. For generating "what grade will I get" — no.

## Bottom line

**After IB exams**, the winning move is quiet preparation: honest band estimates, saved scripts, coordinator on speed dial, and zero leak culture. Results day rewards students with evidence — not students with the most anxious group chat.

Scoring highly in IB English A: Literature HL is not about memorising more — it is about aligning your answers with what examiners reward in the **markbands**. Strategic use of [IB English A: Literature past papers](/ib/past-papers/english-a-literature-hl) under timed conditions, honest self-marking, and targeted feedback closes the gap between a 5 and a 7.

## Understanding the IB English A: Literature HL assessment
**Paper 1** guided analysis of unseen poetry or prose. **Paper 2** comparative essay on studied works. **HL Essay** on a literary line of inquiry. Browse the full subject overview at [IB English A: Literature HL](/ib/subjects/english-a-literature-hl).

## Markbands and what examiners reward
Sustained **literary analysis** — imagery, structure, voice — tied to an arguable thesis.

## A past paper workflow that actually works
Paper 1: tension + technique in the opening paragraph. Paper 2: alternate texts; answer the whole prompt.

## Paper-specific tips
Close read line by line; context only when it serves interpretation.

## Common pitfalls
Paraphrase; memorised essays; HL Essay plot retell.

## Using MarkScheme for targeted feedback

## Frequently asked questions

### Poetry or prose Paper 1?
Either may appear — practise both.

### HL Essay length?
1,200–1,500 words — start after works are well understood.

### Course?
[Literature HL](/ib/courses/english-a-literature-hl) and [SL](/ib/courses/english-a-literature-sl).

Treat [IB English A: Literature HL past papers](/ib/past-papers/english-a-literature-hl) as training for examiner thinking — not a trivia test. Cycle timed practice, honest band marking, and focused drills on your weakest criteria; that is how top candidates make a 7 predictable rather than lucky.

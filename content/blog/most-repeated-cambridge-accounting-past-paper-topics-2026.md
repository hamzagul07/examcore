Accounting revision clicks when you treat papers as **repeatable mark patterns** — ledgers, statements, ratios and variances with clear workings. This guide ranks high-frequency themes across Cambridge **9706** from **2019–2025**.

> **Key takeaway:** **9706** marks come from **accurate figures + labelled statements + shown workings** — silent calculations lose method marks.

## How this list was built

We weighted structured-question frequency, mark-scheme point patterns, and examiner report comments on incomplete financial statements and missing labels.

## A-Level Accounting (9706)

| Priority | Topic | Why it repeats |
|----------|-------|----------------|
| 1 | **Sole trader & partnership accounts** | Income statement, appropriation, capital/current accounts |
| 2 | **Limited company financial statements** | SOCI, SOFP, equity, dividends, reserves |
| 3 | **Adjustments (accruals, prepayments, depreciation)** | Every statement question chain |
| 4 | **Bank reconciliation & control accounts** | Error identification and corrected balances |
| 5 | **Ratio analysis & interpretation** | Liquidity, profitability, gearing — comment in context |
| 6 | **Marginal & absorption costing** | Unit costs, profit comparisons, decision advice |
| 7 | **Standard costing & variances** | Material, labour, fixed overhead variances |
| 8 | **Budgeting & budgetary control** | Cash/working capital budgets; flexed budgets |

## Mark-scheme habits

| Trap | Fix |
|------|-----|
| Missing account labels | Match **mark scheme wording** exactly |
| No workings column | Show **every step** — ECF often applies |
| Ratio without comment | State **what it means for the business** |
| Wrong sign on adjustments | Dr/Cr logic before touching the statement |

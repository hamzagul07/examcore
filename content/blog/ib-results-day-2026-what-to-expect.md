IB Diploma results for the May 2026 exam session are released in early July 2026, marking a pivotal moment for students worldwide. This day is the culmination of two years of hard work, but the process of receiving and interpreting your results—and knowing what to do next—can feel daunting. Expect a straightforward online process for accessing your scores, followed by a clear set of options if your results aren't what you hoped for.

## When and how will I get my results?

Your IB results will be made available to you online in **early July 2026**. The International Baccalaureate Organisation releases results to schools first, and then to students via a dedicated portal. Your school's **IB Coordinator** is your key point of contact; they will confirm the exact date and time of the release and provide you with a unique personal code and PIN. You will use these credentials to log in to the official IB candidate results website (candidates.ibo.org) to view your component grades and final score. Remember to get these details from your coordinator well in advance to avoid any last-minute panic.

## Understanding your IB Diploma score

The IB Diploma is scored out of a maximum of **45 points**. This total is composed of scores from your six subjects and the IB Core. Each of your six chosen subjects is graded on a scale of 1 (lowest) to 7 (highest), making a total of 42 points available from your academic subjects. The remaining three points are awarded based on your performance in the Core components: the **Theory of Knowledge (TOK)** presentation and essay, and the **Extended Essay (EE)**. Your grades in these two elements are combined in a matrix to grant you between 0 and 3 bonus points, which are then added to your subject total to give your final score.

## What do I need to pass the Diploma?

To be awarded the IB Diploma, you must meet a set of specific conditions, not just achieve a certain score. The most well-known requirement is scoring a minimum of **24 points** overall. However, you must also satisfy several other criteria. These include achieving at least 12 points from your three Higher Level (HL) subjects and at least 9 points from your three Standard Level (SL) subjects. Crucially, you must also avoid any "failing conditions," such as receiving a Grade 1 in any subject, getting a Grade E in either the Theory of Knowledge or the Extended Essay, or failing to complete your Creativity, Activity, Service (CAS) requirements.

## My results aren't what I expected – what now?

If your results are lower than anticipated and you've missed your university offer, don't panic. Your first step should be to contact the university's admissions department directly; they may still honour your place, especially if you only narrowly missed the conditions. Your next option is an **Enquiry Upon Results (EUR)**, which is essentially a remark of your work. Discuss this with your IB Coordinator, as your grade can go down as well as up, and there is a fee involved. Finally, you have the option to retake subjects in the November 2026 exam session to improve your score, which can be a viable path for securing a university place the following year.

## How to prepare for the day

While you can't change your grades on the morning of results day, you can prepare for the logistics and potential outcomes. Have your candidate login details ready and ensure you know who to contact at your school if you have questions. It's also wise to have the phone numbers for your first-choice and insurance-choice universities handy. While you wait, familiarising yourself with the exam format and marking criteria can provide context for your results. ## Frequently asked questions

### What is a "failing condition"?

A failing condition is a rule that, if broken, means you are not awarded the IB Diploma, regardless of your total points. Key examples include not achieving at least 24 points, receiving a Grade 1 in any subject, or getting a Grade E for both your Theory of Knowledge and Extended Essay.

### Can my grade go down after a remark?

Yes, it absolutely can. An Enquiry Upon Results (EUR) means your work is re-marked by a senior examiner. The resulting grade can be higher, lower, or remain the same. The new grade is final, even if it is lower than the original, so it's a decision that should be made carefully with your coordinator.

### What's the difference between the Diploma and IB Course Results?

The IB Diploma is the full programme: six subjects, TOK, EE, and CAS, leading to the 45-point score. Some students are registered as Course Results candidates (previously 'Certificates'), meaning they take one or more IB subjects individually but do not complete the full Core. They receive grades for their subjects but not an overall Diploma score.

## Bottom line

IB results day in July 2026 is a significant milestone, but it is not the final word on your academic future. Understanding how your score is calculated and knowing the options available—from contacting universities to requesting remarks or planning for retakes—empowers you to navigate the day successfully. Whatever the outcome, there is always a path forward.

## Quick answer

A free **Revision Village alternative** for IB: official IB past papers and subject reports through your school, free video tutorials and community notes, and **MarkScheme** ([markscheme.app/mark](/mark)) to mark your handwritten answers against IB assessment criteria plus [free IB courses](/ib/courses). Revision Village is freemium — its exam questions, practice exams and video library are mostly behind a subscription.

Revision Village is one of the best-known IB revision sites, especially for Maths and the sciences, thanks to its topic-sorted questions, practice exams and video walkthroughs. It is a strong platform, but most of its depth sits behind a paid plan. You can assemble a free toolkit that covers the same ground by centring your revision on official materials and pairing them with a free marking tool.

## Start with the source: official IB materials

The IB does not release past papers freely online, so ask your teacher or IB coordinator for **past papers, mark schemes and subject reports** in your subjects. These are the most authoritative resources you can revise from: past papers show the real question style, and subject reports reveal the recurring mistakes and what top candidates did differently each session. Pair them with the official assessment criteria for each subject and you have the same "know exactly what examiners reward" advantage that paid platforms sell.

## For topic questions and practice exams

Revision Village's topic-sorted question bank and practice exams are its headline features. You can rebuild this for free: work through past-paper questions grouped by syllabus topic and track them in a simple log by paper, session and question number. MarkScheme's [IB topic practice](/ib/past-papers) surfaces real questions by topic and lets you mark each attempt immediately, so you get the targeted, question-by-topic workflow without a subscription.

## For worked solutions and feedback

Much of Revision Village's value is in its video solutions. The free equivalent is to attempt a question, then mark it carefully against the criteria yourself. MarkScheme helps here: photograph a handwritten answer and it marks it **against the IB assessment criteria and markbands**, criterion by criterion, showing where marks are won and lost. It is deliberately honest about its limits — a fast second pass to build examiner instincts, not a guaranteed grade — so always self-mark against the official criteria first. That habit, using [our marking page](/mark), teaches you *why* a response lands in one markband rather than the next.

## For structured learning

Where Revision Village bundles courses and videos, you can get a structured path for free. Your subject guide is the definitive checklist, and MarkScheme's [free IB courses](/ib/courses) cover all seven subject groups, breaking each topic into short lessons. Combined with the [best free IB revision resources](/blog/best-free-ib-revision-resources-2026), it is a clear, no-cost structure for the full Diploma.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is Revision Village worth the money?
Revision Village bundles topic questions, practice exams and video solutions into a convenient platform that many IB students, especially in Maths, find helpful. But its core value — targeted practice and clear feedback — can be reproduced for free using official past papers, subject reports and a free marking tool that centres on the real assessment criteria.

### How is MarkScheme different from Revision Village?
Revision Village is primarily a question bank and video-solution library. MarkScheme is a marking tool: you submit your own exam answers — including long-answer and essay questions — and it grades them against the IB assessment criteria and markbands, so you learn how the markbands are actually applied. MarkScheme marks against the published criteria, not reconstructed mark schemes, and is transparent about being a second pass rather than a final grade.

### Can I revise for IB Maths for free?
Yes. Use official past papers via your school, follow a free structured path such as MarkScheme's [IB courses](/ib/courses), practise questions by topic, and mark each attempt against the criteria — with MarkScheme's [free marking](/mark) as a quick second pass. This mirrors a paid platform's workflow at no cost.

## Bottom line

Revision Village is a capable platform, but it is not essential for a strong IB result. By combining official IB past papers and subject reports, free community explanations, and the self-marking tools and free courses on MarkScheme, a focused student can build an equally effective, criteria-driven revision system for free. The surest route to top marks is understanding the assessment criteria deeply — and the free, official path gets you there most directly.

The official grade boundaries for the Cambridge O-Level 5090 Biology exams in 2026 will be released on results day, which for the June session is Thursday, 13 August 2026. These boundaries, or 'grade thresholds', are the minimum raw marks you need to achieve a specific grade (A*, A, B, etc.) and are determined only after all exam papers have been marked. This article explains how these boundaries are set and how you can use past data to estimate your performance.

## What are grade boundaries?
Grade boundaries are the minimum raw scores required to achieve each grade for a particular exam paper and for the overall qualification. They are not pre-set percentages; instead, they are carefully calculated each year to reflect the difficulty of the examination. For 5090 Biology, Cambridge sets separate thresholds for each component paper (e.g., Paper 1 Multiple Choice, Paper 2 Theory) and an overall threshold for the complete syllabus. This ensures that the standard required to get, for example, a grade A remains consistent from one year to the next.

## How Cambridge sets the 5090 Biology boundaries
After the exam period, every script from every centre is marked by Cambridge examiners. Senior examiners then analyse student performance across all papers, looking at how candidates responded to each question. They review the overall difficulty of the exam compared to previous years. Based on this expert judgement, they determine the minimum raw mark that represents a grade A performance, a grade C performance, and so on. This process, known as the **awarding** meeting, ensures that a student's final grade reflects their subject understanding, not how easy or hard a particular year's paper was.

## Why do grade boundaries change each year?
Grade boundaries for 5090 Biology fluctuate between exam sessions for one primary reason: to ensure fairness. The difficulty of exam papers inevitably varies slightly from year to year. If a paper is judged to be more challenging than the previous year's, the grade boundaries will likely be lowered to compensate. Conversely, if a paper proves to be more straightforward, the boundaries may be raised. This adjustment ensures that you are not disadvantaged by an unusually difficult paper or advantaged by an easier one, maintaining the integrity and standard of the O-Level qualification over time.

## How component papers affect your overall grade
The Cambridge 5090 Biology syllabus is assessed through a combination of component papers, typically including a multiple-choice paper, a structured theory paper, and a practical assessment. Each of these components tests different skills and knowledge from across the syllabus, from cell structures and plant physiology to genetics and ecology. Each paper is assigned a **weighting factor** that determines its contribution to your final overall mark. You can find detailed information on the specific papers and their weightings on the main [5090 Biology page](/subjects/5090).

## How to estimate your 5090 grade before results day
While you cannot know the 2026 boundaries in advance, you can make a reasonable estimation of your performance. The best method is to practise full past papers under timed, exam-like conditions. Once completed, you should [mark your paper](/mark) strictly using the official Cambridge mark scheme, paying close attention to the required keywords. After calculating your raw score for each component, you can compare these scores against the published grade boundaries from the last two or three available exam sessions. ### Where will the official 2026 grade boundaries be published?
Cambridge International will publish the definitive grade thresholds for the June 2026 session on their public website on results day, 13 August 2026. ### Is it true that practical papers have lower boundaries?
This is a common myth. While the raw mark boundaries for practical papers can sometimes appear lower than for theory papers, this is not a rule. It simply reflects the performance of the cohort in that specific session. Practical skills are different from theoretical knowledge, and boundary setting accounts for this.

### Can I use the 2024 or 2025 boundaries to predict my 2026 grade?
You can use them as a *guide*, but not as a concrete prediction. Past boundaries are the best available data for gauging your performance level. By comparing your raw scores from practice papers against recent thresholds, you get a solid, evidence-based estimation of the grade you are currently working at.

### Why is using correct terminology so important for my marks?
The 5090 Biology mark schemes are very specific. Examiners look for precise **keywords** and scientific terms from the syllabus. You might understand a concept perfectly, but if you fail to use the required term in your answer—for example, writing 'energy-making' instead of 'respiration'—you will likely not be awarded the mark.

## Bottom line
The exact grade boundaries for 5090 Biology 2026 remain unknown until results day. They are dynamic and are set after marking to ensure consistent standards year on year. Your focus should be on mastering the syllabus content, learning the key terminology, and consistently practising with past papers to improve your raw score, as this is the only factor you can control.

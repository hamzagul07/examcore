Scoring highly in IB Economics HL is not about memorising more — it is about aligning your answers with what examiners reward in the **markbands**. Strategic use of [IB Economics past papers](/ib/past-papers/economics-hl) under timed conditions, honest self-marking, and targeted feedback closes the gap between a 5 and a 7.

## Understanding the IB Economics HL assessment
**Paper 1** extended essays. **Paper 2** data response from an extract. **Paper 3** (HL) policy and quantitative questions. **IA** commentaries. Browse the full subject overview at [IB Economics HL](/ib/subjects/economics-hl).

## Markbands and what examiners reward
Essays need definitions, diagrams, analysis chains, and **evaluation** with a justified judgement.

## A past paper workflow that actually works
Essay plan → two diagram chains → evaluation paragraph. Paper 3: practise quantitative drills separately from essays.

## Paper-specific tips
Draw labelled diagrams and explain them in words. Paper 2: quote the extract for application marks.

## Common pitfalls
Descriptive essays; ignoring Paper 3; weak IA commentaries; no evaluation on part (d).

## Using MarkScheme for targeted feedback
Self-marking against band descriptors is essential, but extended responses benefit from a second opinion. After a past paper or IA section, [get criterion-based feedback](/mark?subject=ib-economics-hl) aligned with IB assessment objectives — the same habits that lift exam scripts also sharpen coursework drafts.

# Community seed threads — official, honest starter content

These are **official MarkScheme Team** threads to kick off the Exam Room community. They are the platform's own voice — a welcome + open prompts that invite **real** students to reply. They are **not** fake student posts and must not be presented as peer activity.

## How to post them (the honest way)

1. Sign in with a **designated official/team account** whose username is unmistakably the team (e.g. `MarkScheme_Team` / `MarkScheme` / `ExamRoom_Team`) — not a student-looking handle.
2. Go to `/community/submit`, pick the board + subject, and paste the title/body below.
3. Pin the Welcome thread if the board supports pinning; leave the prompt threads open for replies.
4. Do **not** create additional accounts to reply to these — let genuine replies build. Seeding the *prompts* is fine; seeding fake *answers* is not.

## 1. Welcome (pin) — board: any / subject: general or most-active

**Title:** Welcome to the Exam Room 👋 — how this community works

**Body:**
This is a space for IB and Cambridge students to help each other — ask a doubt, share what's working, or post an answer for a second opinion.

A few things worth knowing:
- **Ask anything** — a tricky topic, a past-paper question, an IA idea. No question is too small.
- **Every question can also be marked.** Upload your answer and get mark-by-mark feedback from the marker, then bring the tricky bits back here to discuss.
- **Be kind and specific.** Say the subject, the topic, and what you've tried — you'll get better help.
- **Share, don't just take.** If a resource or tip helped you, post it so the next person finds it faster.

New here? Start with a prompt from the empty-state cards, or just reply below and tell us what you're studying. 👇

## 2. Prompt — Ask your doubts (board: ib, subject: general)

**Title:** 📌 Stuck on a topic? Ask your subject doubts here

**Body:**
Drop your question below — the subject, the topic, and where you're stuck. Other students (and we) will help.
If it's a past-paper question, you can also get your answer marked for instant feedback, then post the parts you want to talk through. What's tripping you up this week?

## 3. Prompt — Share a resource or study tip (board: any, subject: general)

**Title:** 📚 What resource or study tip is actually working for you?

**Body:**
We all have one — a video that finally made a topic click, a way of revising that stuck, a past-paper habit that moved your grade. Share it below so the next student finds it faster. Include the subject and level so people can filter.

## 4. Prompt — Hardest topic (board: ib, subject: general)

**Title:** Which IB topic are you finding hardest right now?

**Body:**
Name the subject and the topic. If enough people are stuck on the same thing, we'll point you to the lesson + practice that fixes it — and you might find someone here who's already cracked it.

- ✅ Official welcome + open prompts under a clearly-labeled team account.
